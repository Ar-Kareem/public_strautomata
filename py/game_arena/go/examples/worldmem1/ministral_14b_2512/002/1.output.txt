### Strategy for This Policy

This policy is designed to be a **strong but computationally efficient** Go AI that combines **pattern-based opening moves**, **basic territory control**, and **simple ko threat detection**. The strategy has three main phases:

1. **Opening Phase (First ~30 moves):**
   - Follows a **standard 3-4 point opening** (similar to the "Approximate Opening" strategy) to quickly establish a balanced position.
   - Uses **fixed patterns** for the first few moves to ensure consistency and avoid blunders.
   - Avoids playing in the corners too early unless the opponent does.

2. **Middle Game (After ~30 moves):**
   - Switches to a **territory-based strategy**, prioritizing moves that maximize potential territory while maintaining balance.
   - Uses **simple liberties counting** to avoid unnecessary self-atari.
   - Avoids playing near opponent groups with too few liberties unless it’s a clear threat.

3. **Endgame (Late Game):**
   - Focuses on **securing territory** and **filling eyes** while avoiding unnecessary ko fights.
   - Uses **passing** when the board is mostly filled and no clear move stands out.

### Key Features:
- **Fixed Opening:** Ensures the AI doesn’t blunder in the opening.
- **Territory Maximization:** Prioritizes moves that expand influence.
- **Ko Threat Detection:** Avoids playing near opponent groups with 1 liberty unless it’s a clear threat.
- **Efficiency:** Uses **bitmask representations** and **precomputed patterns** to stay within the 1-second constraint.
