The strategy for this policy is to implement a sophisticated Go AI using a combination of heuristic evaluation and local tactical patterns. The policy prioritizes:

1. **Efficient Search**: Rather than deep minimax (too slow for 19x19), we use a fast, rule-based heuristic that evaluates liberties, captures, and vital points.
2. **Liberty Counting**: Prioritize moves that maximize our liberties while minimizing opponent liberties.
3. **Immediate Captures**: Always play a move that captures opponent stones if available.
4. **Shape and Influence**: Favor moves that build thickness, connect own stones, and surround opponent groups.
5. **Ko Prevention**: Track potential ko fights using memory.
6. **Corner-Edge-Center Priority**: In early game, prioritize corners (highest efficiency), then edges, then center.
7. **Territory Evaluation**: Score potential territory using a simplified Lee-style evaluation.
8. **Safety First**: Avoid moves that create suicidal groups unless capturing.
9. **Memory Utilization**: Store ko threats and recent board states to avoid repetition and identify tactical patterns.

To stay under 1 second, we limit evaluation to moves near existing stones (within 2 spaces) and use a fast set-based board representation. We avoid full-board scanning by using neighbor exploration and group-based liberty counting. If no good moves exist, we pass.
