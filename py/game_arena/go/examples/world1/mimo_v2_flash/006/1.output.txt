**Strategy for the Go Playing AI**

My strategy for the 19x19 Go game is a heuristic-based approach designed for efficiency and effectiveness. It prioritizes survival and territorial gain through a weighted evaluation of the board state.

1.  **Liberty Detection and Group Stability:** I first construct a map of the board using sets for quick lookups. I then implement a flood-fill (DFS) algorithm to identify connected groups of stones for both `me` and `opponent`. Crucially, I count the liberties (empty adjacent points) for these groups.
2.  **Critical Defense:** My highest priority is to prevent my own groups from being captured. I scan `my_groups` for any group with exactly 1 liberty. If found, I play at that liberty to save the group.
3.  **Greedy Capture:** Next, I look for opportunities to capture the opponent. I scan `opponent_groups` for any group with exactly 1 liberty. If found, I play at that liberty to capture the opponent's stones (and the surrounding empty space).
4.  **Territorial Expansion:** If no immediate life-or-death situations exist, I look to expand my territory. I evaluate all legal empty points based on how many of my own stones they connect to. Playing on a point that connects to the most friendly stones maximizes influence and group cohesion.
5.  **Passing:** If no beneficial moves are found (e.g., the board is full or the AI is forced to pass to avoid suicide), the AI returns `(0, 0)`.

This logic ensures the AI actively fights for survival and capture while maintaining connected, strong groups during the mid-game.
