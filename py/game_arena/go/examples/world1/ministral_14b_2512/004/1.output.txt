### **Strategy for this Go Policy (19x19 Board)**
This policy aims to **balance territorial control, influence, and local strength** while adapting to the opponent’s moves. The key principles are:

1. **Opening Phase (First ~20 moves):**
   - Play **fuzzy openings** (e.g., 3-4 point openings) to maximize flexibility.
   - Prioritize **high-value board positions** (corners, sides, and key 3-3/3-4 points).
   - Avoid overly rigid patterns; prefer **semi-open moves** (e.g., 3-4, 4-4) over standard joseki.

2. **Middle Game (~20-80 moves):**
   - **Expand territory** by filling large empty spaces near your stones.
   - **Attack weak opponent groups** while defending your own.
   - **Balance influence**—avoid overcommitting to one side; maintain options elsewhere.
   - **Use liberties efficiently**—if a move creates a potential threat (e.g., a 2-space jump), consider it.

3. **Endgame (Last ~80 moves):**
   - **Secure seki (deadlocks)** if they benefit your overall position.
   - **Fill eyes** (single-point enclosures) to ensure life.
   - **Avoid unnecessary fights**—focus on reducing opponent’s potential while maximizing your own.

4. **General Rules:**
   - **Never play illegally** (e.g., into suicide, off-board, or ko violations).
   - **Pass when no clear move exists** (e.g., in a fully filled board or when all moves are weak).
   - **Prioritize board center** (rows 7-13, cols 7-13) for influence.
   - **Avoid playing in opponent’s large territories** unless it forces a critical response.

5. **Evaluation Metrics (Simplified):**
   - **Territory:** Prefer moves that enclose more empty space.
   - **Influence:** Prefer moves near opponent’s stones that disrupt their potential.
   - **Liberty Control:** Avoid moves that give opponent a large local advantage.
   - **Board Balance:** Distribute moves across the board to prevent opponent from dominating one side.

6. **Move Selection:**
   - **If opponent has a weak group (1-2 liberties):** Capture it immediately.
   - **If my group is weak (1-2 liberties):** Defend it with a move that maintains balance.
   - **Otherwise:** Play a **territorial or influence move** (e.g., extending, jumping, or filling).
   - **If no strong move exists:** Pass (`(0, 0)`).

        0.3 * influence_score +

def compute_territory(board: np.ndarray, r: int, c: int) -> float:
