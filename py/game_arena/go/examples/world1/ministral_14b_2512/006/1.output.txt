The strategy for this policy is a **hybrid approach** combining **territory-based influence**, **stone placement efficiency**, and **basic pattern recognition** to make strong moves while respecting the 1-second constraint. Here's the breakdown:

### **Key Principles of the Strategy**
1. **Influence & Territory Control**
   - Prioritize moves that maximize potential territory (e.g., near corners or edges).
   - Avoid overplaying in the center unless necessary for balance.

2. **Stone Placement Efficiency**
   - Place stones where they have the highest **liberty advantage** (securing future liberties while limiting opponent’s).
   - Prefer **solid placements** (moves with 3+ liberties) over risky ones.

3. **Basic Pattern Recognition**
   - If the opponent has a **large unbalanced shape**, attack it (e.g., by cutting or reducing liberties).
   - If the opponent has a **single stone with 1 liberty**, capture it (if legal).
   - Avoid **ko violations** (check if the move would repeat the last board state).

4. **Balance & Center Control**
   - If the board is **empty or nearly empty**, play in the **center** (e.g., 3-3, 9-9) to maintain balance.
   - If the board is **crowded**, look for **sente opportunities** (moves that force the opponent to respond).

5. **Passing When Necessary**
   - If no strong move is found, **pass** (return `(0, 0)`).
