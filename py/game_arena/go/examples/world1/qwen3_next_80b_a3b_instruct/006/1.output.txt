The strategy for this policy is to implement a balanced approach combining pattern recognition, influence evaluation, and basic life-and-death reasoning. The policy prioritizes:

1. Capturing opponent groups with only one liberty left (immediate threat)
2. Completing my own groups to ensure life (two eyes or potential for two eyes)
3. Playing at vital points to prevent opponent from forming eyes
4. Taking center and corner influence points when no immediate tactical moves exist
5. Passing only when the board is completely filled or no beneficial moves remain

The algorithm uses a layered approach:
- First, check for immediate captures (opponent groups with 1 liberty)
- Then, check for my own groups that can be completed to secure life
- Then, evaluate opponent's potential eye spaces and play at vital points
- Finally, use a weighted influence map favoring center and symmetry to choose moves
