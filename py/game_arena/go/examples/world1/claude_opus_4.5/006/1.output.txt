## Strategy

My Go policy will use a combination of strategic principles:

1. **Opening Strategy**: Prioritize corner positions (star points and 3-4 points), then edges, focusing on key strategic points.

2. **Capture Detection**: Identify opponent groups with only one liberty and capture them immediately.

3. **Self-Preservation**: Avoid moves that would put my own groups in atari (one liberty) unless necessary, and save groups that are in atari.

4. **Territory and Influence**: Evaluate moves based on:
   - Proximity to existing stones (for connection/influence)
   - Control of key areas
   - Blocking opponent expansion

5. **Pattern Recognition**: Simple patterns for common situations like:
   - Extending from existing stones
   - Blocking opponent approaches
   - Creating/preventing eyes

6. **Legal Move Validation**: Ensure all moves are legal (not occupied, not suicide unless capturing).

7. **Monte Carlo Element**: For complex positions, use light random playouts to evaluate candidate moves within time constraints.

The policy prioritizes:
1. Capturing opponent stones in atari
2. Saving own stones in atari
3. Strategic opening moves
4. Moves that maximize influence while limiting opponent territory

def policy(me: list[tuple[int, int]], opponent: list[tuple[int, int]]) -> tuple[int, int]:

                    stack.append((nr, nc))

                    liberties.add((nr, nc))
        return group, liberties
