To solve this problem, we need to design a chess AI policy that selects the best legal move based on standard chess objectives. The policy should prioritize checkmate if available, then winning material, improving king safety, and preventing immediate threats. The solution involves evaluating each possible move using a heuristic scoring system and selecting the move with the highest score.

### Approach
1. **Checkmate Detection**: Moves that end in checkmate ('#') are assigned the highest score (1000000) as they immediately win the game.
2. **Promotion Moves**: Moves involving pawn promotion are evaluated based on the piece type (queen, rook, bishop, or knight). If the promotion includes a capture ('x'), the score is the promotion piece's value plus 1000. If no capture is involved, the score is just the promotion piece's value.
3. **Captures**: Moves that capture an opponent's piece ('x') are assigned a score of 1000.
4. **Castling**: Castling moves (O-O or O-O-O) are assigned a score of 500 to indicate their importance for king safety.
5. **King Moves**: Moves starting with 'K' (king moves) are assigned a low score (1) as they are generally avoided unless necessary.
6. **Other Moves**: All other moves are assigned a score of 10.
7. **Best Move Selection**: The move with the highest score is selected. If multiple moves have the same score, the first one encountered is chosen.

### Solution Code
```python
def policy(pieces, to_play, legal_moves, memory):
    scores = {}
    promo_values = {
        'Q': 900,
        'R': 500,
        'B': 300,
        'N': 300
    }
    
    for move in legal_moves:
        if move.endswith('#'):
            score = 1000000
        elif '=' in move:
            promo_part = move.split('=')[1]
            promo_piece = promo_part[0] if promo_part else None
            promo_value = promo_values.get(promo_piece, 0)
            if 'x' in move:
                score = 1000 + promo_value
            else:
                score = promo_value
        elif 'x' in move:
            score = 1000
        elif move in ['O-O', 'O-O-O']:
            score = 500
        elif move.startswith('K'):
            score = 1
        else:
            score = 10
        
        scores[move] = score
    
    best_move = max(legal_moves, key=lambda m: scores[m])
    return (best_move, memory)
```

### Explanation
1. **Checkmate Handling**: The algorithm first checks if any move ends with '#', which indicates checkmate. Such moves are given the highest priority (score 1000000).
2. **Promotion Handling**: For moves involving promotions (denoted by '='), the algorithm extracts the promoted piece's value (queen, rook, bishop, or knight). If the promotion is part of a capture (contains 'x'), the score is the piece's value plus 1000. For non-capture promotions, the score is the piece's value.
3. **Capture Handling**: Moves that capture an opponent's piece (marked by 'x') are assigned a score of 1000 to prioritize material gain.
4. **Castling Handling**: Castling moves (O-O or O-O-O) are assigned a score of 500 to reflect their role in enhancing king safety.
5. **King Moves**: Moves starting with 'K' (king moves) are assigned a low score (1) since they are generally avoided unless necessary for escape from check.
6. **Default Handling**: All other moves receive a base score of 10, ensuring minimal priority unless they meet the criteria above.
7. **Best Move Selection**: The move with the highest score is selected. In case of ties, the first move encountered is chosen.

This approach efficiently evaluates possible moves using a prioritized scoring system, ensuring the AI makes optimal decisions based on chess heuristics within the given constraints.
