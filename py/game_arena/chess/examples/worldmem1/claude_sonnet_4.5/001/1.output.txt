I'll create a strong chess policy that evaluates positions and selects the best moves based on material advantage, piece positioning, king safety, and tactical opportunities.

## Strategy

My policy will use the following approach:

1. **Immediate Win Detection**: Check for checkmate moves first
2. **Material Evaluation**: Calculate material balance with standard piece values
3. **Positional Evaluation**: 
   - Use piece-square tables to reward good piece placement
   - Evaluate king safety (especially in middlegame)
   - Reward central control and piece activity
4. **Tactical Awareness**:
   - Prioritize captures that win material
   - Detect checks that may lead to tactical advantages
   - Avoid hanging pieces when possible
5. **Move Simulation**: For each legal move, simulate the resulting position and evaluate it
6. **Opening Principles**: In early game, prioritize development and center control

The evaluation function combines material count with positional bonuses, using piece-square tables adapted from standard chess engines.
