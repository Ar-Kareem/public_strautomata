### Strategy for This Policy

The goal of this policy is to create a **strong, rule-based chess AI** that prioritizes key chess principles while remaining computationally efficient (within the 1-second constraint). The strategy is inspired by **minimax with alpha-beta pruning** but simplified to avoid excessive computation. Here's the breakdown:

1. **Checkmate First**: If any move in `legal_moves` results in checkmate, select it immediately.
2. **Material Gain**: Prefer moves that capture opponent pieces (especially high-value pieces like Queen > Rook > Bishop > Knight > Pawn).
3. **King Safety**: Avoid moves that expose the king to checks or tactical threats (e.g., moving the king into danger).
4. **Development & Control**: Prioritize moves that develop pieces (e.g., pawn moves, knight/bishop outposts) and control the center.
5. **Tactical Opportunities**: Look for pins, forks, skewers, or discovered attacks.
6. **Pawn Structure**: Avoid weakening pawn structures (e.g., isolated pawns, doubled pawns) unless necessary.
7. **Castling**: Prefer castling if it improves king safety and doesn't block development.
8. **Promotions**: Always promote to the highest-value piece (Queen) unless a tactical reason exists otherwise.
9. **Disambiguation**: Use disambiguation (e.g., `Nec3`) to avoid ambiguity in captures or checks.
10. **Fallback**: If no clear tactical or positional advantage is found, choose a move that maintains material balance and doesn't lose tempo.

### Implementation Approach
- **Evaluate Moves**: Assign scores to moves based on material gain, king safety, and positional factors.
- **Prioritize Captures**: Captures are scored higher than non-captures, with higher scores for higher-value pieces.
- **Avoid Blunders**: Never make a move that loses material unless forced.
- **Memory**: Use `memory` to store board state or evaluation caches (though minimal use is made here to keep it simple).
