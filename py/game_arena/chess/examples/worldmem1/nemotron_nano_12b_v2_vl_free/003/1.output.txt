2. **Capture High-Value Pieces**: If no checkmate move is available, the next priority is to capture the highest-value opponent piece. This involves analyzing each legal move to determine if it captures an opponent's piece and selecting the move that captures the most valuable piece.
3. **Positional Moves**: If no capturing moves are available, the policy evaluates moves based on the positional value. Knights and bishops are prioritized to move to central squares, which typically offers better control of the board. Pawns are evaluated based on their movement towards higher ranks, as advancing pawns control key squares.
