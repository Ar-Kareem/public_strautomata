The policy I'll implement uses a heuristic evaluation function to score each legal move, then selects the move with the highest score. The evaluation considers:
1. Checkmate threats (highest priority)
2. Capturing material (queen=9, rook=5, bishop=3, knight=3, pawn=1)
3. Attacking the opponent's king
4. Pawn advancement
5. Piece mobility and positioning
6. King safety (avoiding moves that weaken king position)
7. Avoiding illegal moves with immediate losses

The policy prioritizes moves that lead to checkmate first, then captures of high-value pieces, followed by tactical threats, and finally strategic positional improvements.
