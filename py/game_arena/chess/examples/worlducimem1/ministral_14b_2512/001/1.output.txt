### Strategy for This Policy

This policy will implement a **hybrid approach** combining **evaluation-based move ordering** with **basic tactical patterns** to make strong decisions efficiently within the time constraints. The strategy focuses on:

1. **Checkmate Detection**: If any move leads to an immediate checkmate, prioritize it.
2. **Material Advantage**: Prefer moves that capture opponent pieces (especially high-value ones like queen/rook) while avoiding unnecessary material loss.
3. **King Safety**: Avoid moves that expose the king to checks or tactical threats (e.g., moving the king into danger).
4. **Development & Central Control**: Prioritize moves that develop pieces, control the center, and open lines for rooks/queens.
5. **Pawn Structure**: Prefer moves that improve pawn structure (e.g., avoiding isolated/doubled pawns, supporting passed pawns).
6. **Tactical Patterns**: Look for simple tactics like forks, pins, skewers, and discovered attacks.
7. **Efficiency**: Use a **lightweight evaluation function** and **move ordering heuristics** to quickly narrow down strong candidates without deep search.

The policy will:
- Use a **static evaluation function** to score positions (material + mobility + king safety + pawn structure).
- **Sort legal moves** by a heuristic combining tactical opportunities and positional improvements.
- **Avoid blunders** by checking for immediate threats (e.g., opponent's checks or captures).
- **Cache evaluations** in `memory` to speed up repeated positions.
