The strategy for this policy is to implement a smart chess move selection algorithm that prioritizes:

1. **Immediate Checkmate**: Look for any move that leads to checkmate immediately.
2. **Capturing Pieces**: Prefer moves that capture enemy pieces, especially high-value ones (Queen > Rook > Bishop > Knight > Pawn).
3. **Positional Advantages**: Prefer moves that develop pieces, control center squares, or improve king safety.
4. **Avoiding Immediate Threats**: Avoid moves that leave the king exposed or allow the opponent to capture material immediately.
5. **Pawn Structure**: Prefer moves that support pawn advancement or avoid weak pawn structures.
6. **Promotion**: Prefer moves that lead to pawn promotion when possible.

To implement this:
- I'll use a scoring system for each legal move combining:
  - Material gain from captures
  - King safety considerations
  - Piece development (moving pieces toward center or control key squares)
  - Center control
  - Avoiding captures or moves that expose the king
- In case of tie scores, I'll prefer:
  - Moves toward the center
  - Moves that maintain or improve position
- To calculate the best move:
  1. Iterate through all legal moves
  2. Score each move based on its immediate benefits
  3. Return the move with the highest score

The memory dictionary will be used to store starting position and potentially piece movement history for advanced strategy, but it's not required for this implementation and can be ignored or used for depth-limited search later.
