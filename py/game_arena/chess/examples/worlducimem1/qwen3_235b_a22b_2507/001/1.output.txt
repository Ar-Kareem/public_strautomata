The strategy for this policy is to implement a greedy, depth-1 search with a piece-value heuristic and threat/defense awareness. The AI will:

1. **Evaluate material gain**: Prioritize captures that win material, using standard piece values (Pawn=1, Knight=Bishop=3, Rook=5, Queen=9).
2. **Prevent losing material**: If the opponent can capture a piece on the next move (i.e. our piece is undefended and under attack), avoid moves that don't address that threat—unless we deliver checkmate or gain decisive material.
3. **Prioritize checkmate**: If a move leads to immediate checkmate (detected by checking if the opponent has no legal moves after our move), it is taken.
4. **Favor development and center control**: Slight positional bonuses for knights and bishops moving toward the center.
5. **Promotion incentive**: Strongly favor pawn moves that promote, especially to queen.
6. **Use memory**: Store the previous move to help with move ordering or state tracking (though in this version it's unused beyond being passed back).

The policy scans all legal moves, simulates each one, and scores them based on:
- Immediate material gain (capture value)
- Whether it gives check or checkmate
- Whether it prevents own piece from being captured
- Pawn promotion
- Positional improvement (center control bonus)

It avoids complex recursion due to time constraints but uses smart prioritization to simulate a more advanced player.
