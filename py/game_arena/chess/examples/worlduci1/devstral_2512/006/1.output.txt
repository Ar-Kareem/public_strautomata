### Strategy for the Chess Policy

The strategy for this policy is to prioritize moves that lead to immediate checkmate if available. If no checkmate is possible, the policy will focus on capturing the opponent's pieces to gain a material advantage. If no captures are available, the policy will prioritize moves that improve the position of the king (e.g., moving the king to a safer square or castling). If none of these are applicable, the policy will default to a simple heuristic that prioritizes central control and piece development.

### Key Components of the Strategy:
1. **Checkmate Priority**: Always check if any legal move results in checkmate. If so, return that move immediately.
2. **Material Advantage**: If no checkmate is available, prioritize moves that capture the opponent's pieces, especially high-value pieces like the queen or rook.
3. **King Safety**: If no captures are available, prioritize moves that improve the king's safety, such as castling or moving the king away from threats.
4. **Central Control and Development**: If none of the above apply, prioritize moves that control the center of the board or develop pieces to more active squares.
