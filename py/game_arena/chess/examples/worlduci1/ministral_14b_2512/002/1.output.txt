### **Strategy for this Policy**
This policy will implement a **basic but aggressive chess AI** that prioritizes:
1. **Checkmate** – If any legal move results in a checkmate, it will choose the fastest one.
2. **Material Gain** – If no checkmate is available, it will prefer moves that capture opponent pieces (especially high-value ones like queens, rooks, or bishops).
3. **King Safety** – If the king is in danger (e.g., exposed to multiple pawn attacks or underdeveloped), it will prioritize castling or moving the king to a safer square.
4. **Pawn Promotion** – If a pawn can reach the 8th rank, it will promote to a **queen** (unless blocked by a stronger opponent move).
5. **Development & Central Control** – If no immediate threats or checkmates exist, it will prioritize moving pieces to control the center (e3, e4, d4, d5) and develop knights/bishops early.
6. **Forks & Pins** – If a move creates a fork (attacking two pieces) or a pin (protecting a piece while attacking another), it will favor that move.
7. **Check & Attack** – If the opponent’s king is in check, it will respond with the best legal move to escape or capture. Otherwise, it will look for moves that put the opponent’s king in check.
8. **Avoiding Weak Moves** – If no strong move is found, it will default to a **simple pawn push** (e.g., e4, d4) or **knight/bishop development** (e.g., Nf3, Bb5).

The AI will use **evaluation heuristics** (piece values, king safety, pawn structure) to rank legal moves and select the best one. If no clear advantage is found, it will make a **positionally sound move** (e.g., developing pieces, controlling center).
