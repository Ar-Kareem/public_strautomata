### Strategy for This Policy

The strategy for this policy is a **hybrid approach** combining **evaluation-based move selection** with **pattern recognition** and **basic tactical awareness**. Here's the breakdown:

1. **Checkmate Detection**: If any legal move results in checkmate, prioritize it immediately (highest priority).
2. **Material Advantage**: Prefer moves that maximize material gain (captures with higher piece values).
3. **King Safety**: Avoid moves that expose the king to checks or tactical threats (e.g., moving the king into danger).
4. **Development & Central Control**: For opening/middlegame, prioritize moves that develop pieces, control the center, and avoid blocking pawns.
5. **Pawn Structure**: Prefer moves that improve pawn structure (e.g., passing pawns, avoiding isolated/doubled pawns).
6. **Tactical Opportunities**: Look for simple tactics like forks, pins, and skewers.
7. **Fallback to Evaluation**: If no clear tactical or positional move stands out, use a **static evaluation function** (material + positional bonuses) to pick the best move.

The evaluation function will assign scores based on:
- Material (piece values: P=1, N=3, B=3, R=5, Q=9, K=∞).
- Positional bonuses (e.g., center control, king safety, pawn structure).
- Mobility (number of legal moves for pieces).

The policy will:
1. First check for checkmates.
2. Then evaluate all legal moves using the evaluation function.
3. Select the move with the highest score, breaking ties randomly.
