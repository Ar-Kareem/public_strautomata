### Strategy for This Policy

The strategy for this policy is a **hybrid approach** combining **evaluation-based move selection** with **pattern recognition** and **basic tactical awareness**. Here's the breakdown:

1. **Checkmate Detection**: If any legal move results in an immediate checkmate, prioritize it (highest priority).
2. **Material Advantage**: Prefer moves that increase material advantage (captures opponent's pieces while minimizing losses).
3. **King Safety**: Avoid moves that expose the king to checks or tactical threats (e.g., moving the king into danger).
4. **Development & Control**: Prioritize moves that develop pieces (e.g., knights to center, castling), control the center, and open lines for rooks/queens.
5. **Pawn Structure**: Avoid weakening pawn structure (e.g., isolated/doubled pawns) unless necessary.
6. **Promotion**: If a pawn can promote, prioritize it (especially to queen unless a tactical reason exists).
7. **Randomness for Uncertainty**: If no clear best move is found, introduce controlled randomness to avoid predictable play.

The policy uses a **custom evaluation function** to score moves based on material, mobility, king safety, and positional factors. It also includes **basic tactical checks** (e.g., fork, pin, skewer) to avoid blunders.
