### Strategy for This Policy

This policy will implement a **hybrid evaluation approach** combining **material balance, mobility, king safety, and tactical opportunities** to make strong moves. The strategy prioritizes:

1. **Checkmate in one move** – If any legal move results in checkmate, choose it immediately.
2. **Material advantage** – Prefer moves that maximize material gain (captures) while minimizing loss.
3. **King safety** – Avoid moves that expose the king to unnecessary threats (e.g., castling if safe).
4. **Development & mobility** – Prioritize moves that improve piece activity (e.g., knights/bishops to strong squares).
5. **Pawn structure** – Avoid weakening pawns unnecessarily (e.g., pawn moves that create isolated/doubled pawns).
6. **Tactical threats** – Look for forks, pins, and discovered attacks that can disrupt the opponent.
7. **Center control** – Prefer moves that occupy or control central squares (d4, d5, e4, e5).
8. **Promotions** – Always promote to the highest-value piece (queen) unless a tactical reason suggests otherwise.

The policy will use **evaluation functions** to score moves and select the best one. For efficiency, it will:
- Use **bitboard-like logic** (via `numpy`) for fast piece movement checks.
- Implement **basic tactical detection** (e.g., forks, pins).
- Use **material tables** to quickly assess capture value.
- Apply **heuristics** for king safety and pawn structure.

The code will be optimized to run within the 1-second constraint by avoiding deep search and focusing on **static evaluation** with tactical checks.
