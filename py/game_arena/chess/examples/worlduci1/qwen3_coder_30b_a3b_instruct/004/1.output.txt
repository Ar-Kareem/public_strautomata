The strategy for this policy is to implement a simple but effective chess move selection policy that prioritizes:

1. Checkmate: If a move leads to checkmate, play it immediately.
2. Capture: If a move captures a piece, prefer capturing high-value pieces.
3. King safety: Avoid moves that expose the king to immediate capture or check.
4. Development: Prefer moves that develop pieces (knights and bishops) toward the center.
5. Control: Prefer moves that control center squares (d4, d5, e4, e5).
6. Promotion: If a pawn promotion is possible, prioritize queen promotion.
7. Random if no good options: If no clear best move, select randomly among legal moves.

We will not implement a full chess engine but rather use a smart heuristic-based approach that evaluates each move based on these criteria and selects the highest-scoring move.
