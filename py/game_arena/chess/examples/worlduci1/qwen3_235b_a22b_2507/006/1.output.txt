The strategy for this policy is to prioritize strong tactical decisions using a handcrafted evaluation function that considers material value, piece activity, king safety, and positional advantages. The policy evaluates each legal move by simulating the resulting board state and scoring it based on:

1. **Material Balance**: Assigning standard values (Pawn=1, Knight=Bishop=3, Rook=5, Queen=9) and preferring moves that gain material.
2. **Checkmate Detection**: If a move leads to an unavoidable checkmate (i.e., no legal moves for the opponent), it is selected immediately.
3. **Capture Priority**: Among legal moves, captures are favored when they lead to material gain (e.g., capturing a high-value piece with a low-value piece).
4. **Pawn Promotion**: Promoting a pawn to a queen is prioritized if possible.
5. **Positional Improvements**: Moves that control the center or develop pieces are slightly favored.
6. **King Safety**: Avoid moving the king unnecessarily in the opening/midgame and prefer castling when possible.
7. **Defense**: If a piece is under threat, moves that defend or retreat are considered.

To keep execution fast, we avoid deep search trees and instead use a one-ply greedy heuristic with specific prioritization rules. The move with the highest score is selected. This ensures the policy remains within the 1-second limit while still making intelligent, competitive decisions.
