The evaluation considers material balance, piece-square tables for positional strength, and immediate threats like checks and captures. The policy prioritizes moves that gain material (especially queens and rooks), defend under-attacked pieces, improve piece activity, and avoid unnecessary exchanges unless favorable. It uses hand-tuned piece-square tables to encourage better positioning (e.g., knights in the center, rooks on open files), and ensures king safety by discouraging king movement into vulnerable areas early on.
