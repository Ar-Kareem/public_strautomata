Here's a chess policy that implements a basic minimax algorithm with alpha-beta pruning and a simple evaluation function that considers material balance, piece-square tables, and basic positional factors. The strategy prioritizes checkmate opportunities, material gain, king safety, and center control.

Strategy:
1. First check for immediate checkmate opportunities
2. If none, use minimax with alpha-beta pruning (depth=2 for speed) to evaluate positions
3. Evaluation considers:
   - Material balance (weighted piece values)
   - Piece-square tables for better piece placement
   - King safety (castling rights, pawn shield)
   - Pawn structure (doubled, isolated pawns)
   - Mobility (number of legal moves)
   - Center control (occupation of central squares)
