The strategy for this policy is to implement a multi-layered chess evaluation function that prioritizes immediate tactical gains and positional advantages:
1. First, check for any move that delivers checkmate — return it immediately.
2. If no checkmate, look for moves that capture high-value pieces (Queen > Rook > Bishop/Knight > Pawn) — prioritize material gain.
3. If no material gain, prioritize moves that threaten immediate check or capture on the next turn (attacking opponent's pieces or creating forks/pins).
4. If no tactical opportunities, prefer moves that improve king safety (castling if legal and beneficial), centralize pieces, or control key squares.
5. Prefer moves that develop pieces toward the center and avoid moving the same piece twice in the opening unless necessary.
6. For pawn promotions, always promote to Queen unless a Knight promotion leads to immediate checkmate or prevents stalemate.
7. Use a simple heuristic: assign points to piece values (Queen=9, Rook=5, Bishop=3, Knight=3, Pawn=1) and calculate net material gain for each move.
8. If all else is equal, prefer moves with disambiguation that open lines or attack the opponent’s king zone.

This policy avoids speculative sacrifices and focuses on sound, positionally grounded decisions with tactical precision, which is proven effective in competitive chess AI.
