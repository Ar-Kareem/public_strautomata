The strategy for this policy is a heuristic-based approach that prioritizes moves based on their potential impact: it first checks for checkmate (highest priority), then pawn promotions (weighted by promoted piece value), then castling for king safety, then checks, then captures (by material value gain), and finally other moves with minimal score. The heuristic evaluates each legal move using the current board state from the `pieces` dict to assess captures and promotions, and parses the move string to detect special cases like castling or checks. It selects the move with the highest score, breaking ties by choosing the first in the list, ensuring it's both legal and computationally efficient within the 1-second limit.
