### Strategy for This Policy

This policy is designed to be a **strong, tactical chess AI** that prioritizes:
1. **Checkmate in one move** (highest priority).
2. **Capturing opponent's pieces** (material gain), especially high-value pieces (Queen > Rook > Bishop > Knight > Pawn).
3. **Attacking opponent's king** (checks or threats to the king).
4. **Developing pieces** (moving knights and bishops to active squares).
5. **Controlling the center** (moving pawns to e4/e5/d4/d5).
6. **Castling for king safety** (if legal and not already castled).
7. **Promotions** (if available, prefer Queen promotion).
8. **Avoiding blunders** (never move into obvious tactical traps or lose material).

The policy uses a **scoring system** to evaluate moves based on:
- **Material gain** (positive for captures, negative for losing material).
- **Piece activity** (preferring moves that develop pieces or open lines).
- **King safety** (avoiding moves that expose the king).
- **Tactical opportunities** (checks, pins, forks).

The AI also includes **basic opening principles** (e.g., developing knights and bishops early) and **endgame awareness** (e.g., promoting pawns).
