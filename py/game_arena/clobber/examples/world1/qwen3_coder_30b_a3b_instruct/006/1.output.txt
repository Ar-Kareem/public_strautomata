# Strategy

The Clobber game is a two-player strategy game where each player tries to capture opponent pieces by moving onto them. The key strategic elements for an effective policy are:

1. **Capture Priority**: Always prioritize moves that remove opponent pieces when possible, as this reduces their options and can limit their mobility.

2. **Control the Center**: Occupying or controlling central positions provides more movement options and better strategic flexibility.

3. **Mobility**: Moves that preserve or increase the number of available moves for the player are favorable.

4. **Avoid Forced Moves**: Don't make moves that leave the opponent with very few options, as this might lead to a losing position.

My policy will implement the following approach:
- Evaluate all possible moves for the player
- For each move, determine if it captures an opponent piece
- Prioritize capture moves over non-capture moves
- Among capture moves, prefer those that are in central positions or that maximize future mobility
- For non-capture moves, prefer those that don't create isolated pieces
- If no good moves are available, make any legal move

Since the board is 5x6, it's small enough to search efficiently. I'll use a minimax approach with a shallow search depth (1-2 ply) to evaluate move quality, focusing on capturing and mobility.
