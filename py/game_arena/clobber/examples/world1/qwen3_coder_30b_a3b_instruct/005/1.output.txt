# Strategy Explanation

My strategy for the Clobber game is based on a combination of tactical and strategic principles:

1. **Capture Priority**: I prioritize moves that capture opponent pieces, as this reduces their options and can create opportunities for further captures.

2. **Piece Mobility**: I try to keep my pieces in positions where they can make multiple moves, particularly near the center of the board where there are more potential moves.

3. **Corner and Edge Protection**: I avoid leaving pieces in positions where they can be easily trapped in corners or along edges where movement is limited.

4. **Game State Evaluation**: I evaluate the board state to favor positions that maximize my mobility while minimizing my opponent's mobility.

5. **Capture Chains**: I look for situations where a capture can set up a chain reaction or force the opponent into a position with fewer opportunities, especially when I have multiple legal moves.

The key insight is that Clobber is a game of progressive reduction - the more pieces you can remove, the more freedom you gain while limiting your opponent's options. My approach involves:
- Looking for immediate captures
- Choosing moves that preserve or increase mobility
- Avoiding moves that leave pieces in vulnerable positions
- Prioritizing moves in the center of the board where piece movement options are maximized

I will implement a minimax-based approach with alpha-beta pruning to evaluate positions, but with a limited depth to maintain performance within the 1-second constraint. For a final move selection, I'll evaluate all legal moves based on immediate capture potential and board position factors.
