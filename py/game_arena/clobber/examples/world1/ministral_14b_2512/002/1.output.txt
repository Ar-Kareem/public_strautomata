### Thinking Process

**Understanding the Game Clobber:**
Clobber is a two-player abstract strategy game where players take turns moving their pieces to capture opponent pieces. The board is 5x6, and each player has pieces represented by 1s in their respective arrays (`you` and `opponent`). A move consists of moving one of your pieces orthogonally (up, down, left, right) onto an opponent's piece, capturing it. The goal is to be the last player with legal moves.

**Key Observations:**
1. **Legal Moves:** A move is legal if:
   - The starting cell has your piece (`you[row][col] == 1`).
   - The destination cell is within bounds and has the opponent's piece (`opponent[dest_row][dest_col] == 1`).
   - The move is orthogonal (no diagonal moves).
2. **Objective:** The policy should always return a legal move. The strategy can vary from random to sophisticated (e.g., minimizing opponent's future moves, maximizing captures, etc.).
3. **Efficiency:** The policy must run within 1 second, so brute-force or complex simulations may not be feasible for all cases.

**Strategy Selection:**
For simplicity and reliability, I'll implement a **greedy strategy** that prioritizes moves that:
1. Capture the opponent's pieces in a way that maximizes the number of future moves for yourself (i.e., avoid isolating your pieces).
2. Prefer moves that capture opponent pieces that are adjacent to fewer of your pieces (to reduce opponent's future options).
3. If no such preference is clear, pick the first valid move found.

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Iterate over all your pieces (`you`).
2. For each piece, check all 4 orthogonal directions (U, D, L, R).
3. For each direction, check if the destination is within bounds and has an opponent's piece.
4. Among all legal moves, select one based on the greedy criteria (e.g., prioritize moves that leave the opponent with fewer future moves).
5. Return the first valid move found (or the best one based on criteria).

**Edge Cases:**
- If no moves are found (though the problem states this won't happen), return a default move (though this shouldn't occur per the problem statement).
- Ensure the move is always legal and within bounds.

**Code Structure:**
- Use nested loops to iterate over your pieces and directions.
