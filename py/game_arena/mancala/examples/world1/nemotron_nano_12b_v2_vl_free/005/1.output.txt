To implement a **smart policy** for the Kalah game, we need to choose the best possible move from the current state of the board. The goal is to maximize the chances of winning by prioritizing **captures**, **extra moves**, and **strategic seed distribution**.

---

### 🧠 Key Considerations for the Policy

1. **Captures**:
   - If the last seed lands in your house **j** and **j was empty** before your move, and your opponent's corresponding opposite house **(5 - j)** has seeds, you can capture both your seed and your opponent's seeds.
   - This gives a **direct advantage**, so we should prioritize such moves.

2. **Extra Move**:
   - If the last seed lands in your **store**, you get to make another move. This is highly desirable because it gives you an extra turn.
   - Even small gains in store are better than giving the opponent a turn.

3. **Store Seeds**:
   - If neither capture nor extra move is possible, prioritize moves that place more seeds in your store.

---

### ✅ Strategy Summary

- **Priority 1**: Maximize captured seeds.
- **Priority 2**: Move seeds into your store (ends in store).
- **Priority 3**: Otherwise, choose a move that gives the best chance of future gains.

---

### 🧮 Heuristic Scoring

We use a weight-based scoring system:
- **Store gain** (1 point)
- **Capture** (10–1000 points depending on number of seeds)
- **Extra move** (100 points)

---

### 🧩 Python Implementation

```python
def policy(you, opponent):
    """
    Policy to choose the best move in the Kalah game.
    Evaluates all possible moves and selects the one with the highest priority:
        1. Captures (most valuable)
        2. Ends in the store (extra move)
        3. Other moves
    """
    best_move = None
    best_score = -1
    capture_priority = 1000
    store_priority = 100
    extra_priority = 10

    for move in range(6):
        if you[move] == 0:
            continue  # Skip empty houses

        # Simulate the move and get the capture and store info
        captured, store_gain, ends_in_store = simulate_move_scores(you, opponent, move)

        # Heuristic score:
        # Prioritize captures first, then extra move, then store gain
        score = captured * capture_priority + ends_in_store * extra_priority + store_gain

        # Update best move if this move is better
        if score > best_score:
            best_score = score
            best_move = move

    return best_move


def simulate_move_scores(you, opponent, move_i):
    """
    Simulates the move and returns:
    - captured: number of seeds captured
    - store_gain: number of seeds added to the store during this move
    - ends_in_store: whether the move ends with a seed in the player's store
    """
    new_you = list(you)
    new_opponent = list(opponent)
    seeds = new_you[move_i]
    new_you[move_i] = 0
    current_pos = move + 1
    steps_done = 0

    while steps_done < seeds:
        current_pos %= 13  # wrap around the ring
        if current_pos < 7:
            new_you[current_pos] += 1  # plant in your house
        else:
            # Jumping opponent's store (skip it)
            opponent_index = current_pos - 7
            new_opponent[opponent_index] += 1

        steps_done += 1
        current_pos += 1

    # Determine where the last seed was placed
    last_pos = (current_pos - 1) % 13
    ends_in_store = (last_pos == 6)
    captured = 0

    if last_pos < 6:
        j = last_pos
        # Check if house j was empty before the move
        if j != move_i and you[j] == 0:
            # Capture: seeds in your house plus opponent's opposite house
            opp_house = 5 - j
            if opponent[opp_house] > 0:
                captured = 1 + opponent[opp_house]
    else:
        # end in your store: bonus move
        captured = 0

    store_gain = 1 if ends_in_store else 0
    new_you[6] += captured
    return captured, store_gain, ends_in_store
```

---

### 📌 Summary

- **Legality is guaranteed** by checking `you[move] > 0`.
- **Priorities** are weighted to favor **captures**, then **extra move**, then **store gains**.
- **Efficiency** is maintained by simulating the last position and capture conditions without overloading with full game simulation.

This policy is **robust and efficient**, and should perform well in a competitive environment by leveraging seed captures and maximizing turn advantage.

---

### 🧪 Example

Given a move that:
- Ends in your store → score +=100
- Captures 6 seeds → score += 6000
- Total score = 6100

Another move that:
- Ends in your store → +100
- Captures 0 seeds
- Total score = 100

The first move will be chosen.
