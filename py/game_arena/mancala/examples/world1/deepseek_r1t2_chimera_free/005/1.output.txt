
The strategy for this policy is to use a heuristic-driven approach with a shallow lookahead (up to two ply) to evaluate potential moves. The key components are:

1. **Store Difference**: Prioritize moves that maximize the difference between the player's store and the opponent's store.
2. **Extra Moves**: Grant significant bonuses to moves that result in an extra turn, allowing consecutive actions.
3. **Captures**: Award bonuses for capturing opponent's seeds, which directly increases the store difference.
4. **Opponent Modeling**: For moves without extra turns, simulate the opponent's best response to minimize the player's advantage.
5. **House Seeds**: Encourage moves that preserve seeds on the player's side and reduce seeds on the opponent's side.

The heuristic balances immediate gains with potential future outcomes by considering:
- Current store values.
- Seeds in houses.
- Captured seeds.
- Extra moves.
- The opponent's best countermove.

This policy favors sequences that lead to extra moves and captures while mitigating risks posed by the opponent's possible responses.

def evaluate_state(player, opponent):
    store_diff = player[6] - opponent[6]
    your_houses = sum(player[0:6])
    opp_houses = sum(opponent[0:6])
    return store_diff * 100 + your_houses - opp_houses * 2

def policy(you, opponent):
    valid_moves = [i for i in range(6) if you[i] > 0]
