The game of Kalah (Mancala) is a classic game involving both tactical captures and strategic sowing. The board consists of 7 houses per player (6 stores + 1 bin). The game is won by having more seeds in your store than the opponent.

### Strategy Logic

To win against other policies, I will implement an agent that plays **Optimal Mancala** (often called "Advanced Kalah" rules, which maximize skill over luck). The rules are:
1.  **Capture if possible**: If a move results in landing in an empty house on your side, and the opposite opponent's house has seeds, capture both sets into your store. This yields immediate points and reduces opponent's options.
2.  **Free Turn**: If a move ends in your store, take it immediately. This allows for chaining captures or aggressive sowing.
3.  **Block Opponent**: If no capture is possible, try to land in an empty house on your side such that the opponent's next move (if they play optimally) is blocked (i.e., they have no stones or the move doesn't yield points).
4.  **Avoid Giving Free Turns**: If the opponent has a move that ends in their store, avoid making a move that sets that up for them.
5.  **Heuristic Fallback**: Evaluate remaining valid moves based on the resulting score difference (My Store - Opponent Store) and choose the best one.

The Python code below implements this logic efficiently, iterating through legal moves, simulating the outcome, and applying the capture and free turn rules to select the best move.
