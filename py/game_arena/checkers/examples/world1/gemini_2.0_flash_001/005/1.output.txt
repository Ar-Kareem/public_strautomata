The strategy for this policy is as follows:

1. **Mandatory Jumps:** Prioritize moves that capture opponent pieces. This is a fundamental rule of checkers and taking pieces is always advantageous. If multiple captures are possible, implement a simple heuristic to choose the best one (e.g., maximize the number of captured pieces or prioritize capturing kings).
2. **King Safety:** If no captures are available, prioritize moves that improve the safety of kings. Move kings to less exposed positions.
3. **Piece Advancement:** If no captures or king safety moves are available, advance pieces towards the opponent's side of the board, especially along the edges (a good long-term strategy for potential kings).
4. **Avoid Kinging Opponent / Defend the back row:** If there are no captures, try to avoid positions where the opponent can 'easily' king a piece on the next turn. Also, prioritize defending your back row with pieces.
5. **Simple Evaluation Function:** Evaluate each possible move with a simple heuristic that considers piece advantage (kings are more valuable, more pieces is better), advancement (closer to kinging is better), and some positional scoring to avoid the edge or back row.
6. **Random Legal Move (fallback):** If none of the above heuristics lead to specific action, then select a random valid move to always return a legal turn, and avoid time out errors.

def policy(my_men, my_kings, opp_men, opp_kings, color) -> tuple[tuple[int, int], tuple[int, int]]:
