### Strategy for This Policy

The strategy for this policy is a **hybrid approach** combining **evaluation-based decision-making** with **capture prioritization** and **positional awareness**. Here's the breakdown:

1. **Capture Mandatory**: Always prioritize captures if available, as per the rules. The policy will first evaluate all possible captures (including forced captures) and select the best one based on a heuristic evaluation.

2. **Evaluation Function**: The evaluation function considers:
   - **Material Advantage**: Kings are worth more than regular pieces (e.g., 2 points per king, 1 point per man).
   - **Positional Value**: Control of the center and back rows is valuable. Kings near the opponent's back row are more threatening.
   - **Mobility**: Pieces with more legal moves are preferred.
   - **King Safety**: Kings should avoid being trapped or exposed to captures.

3. **Move Selection**:
   - If captures are available, evaluate all possible captures and choose the one with the highest score.
   - If no captures are available, evaluate all possible regular moves and choose the one with the highest score.
   - Prefer moves that develop kings early (e.g., promoting men to kings when possible).
   - Avoid moving pieces into positions where they can be easily captured.

4. **King Promotion**: If a man reaches the opponent's back row, it is automatically promoted to a king (handled by the evaluation function).

5. **Defensive Play**: If the opponent has a significant material advantage, prioritize defensive moves to protect kings and avoid losing pieces.

6. **Randomness for Tie-Breaking**: If multiple moves have the same score, randomly select one to avoid predictable play.
