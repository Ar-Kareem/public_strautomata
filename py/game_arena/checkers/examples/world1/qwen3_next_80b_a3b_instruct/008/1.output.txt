The strategy for this policy is to implement a minimax algorithm with alpha-beta pruning, combined with a sophisticated evaluation function that prioritizes king promotion, piece safety, board control, and mandatory captures. Since captures are mandatory, the policy first checks for any available capture moves and selects the one that maximizes material gain or leads to king promotion. If no captures are available, the policy explores non-capture moves using minimax to a depth of 4, evaluating positions based on:

1. Piece count (men and kings weighted differently — kings are more valuable),
2. Positional advantage (control of center squares, advance toward opponent's back rank),
3. King promotion potential (pieces close to promotion rank are prioritized),
4. Mobility (number of available legal moves),
5. Safety (avoiding edge positions, especially for non-kings).

The board representation considers only dark squares, so moves are only valid to diagonally adjacent dark squares. The policy ensures legality by validating all moves according to standard checkers rules: men move diagonally forward one square (unless capturing), kings move diagonally any number of squares (but only one square for non-captures), and captures must land in an empty square after jumping over an opponent.

The implementation uses efficient precomputation of diagonals and employs iterative deepening within the 1-second time limit. To avoid timeouts, move ordering is prioritized: captures first, then moves toward promotion, then center control, then mobility.
