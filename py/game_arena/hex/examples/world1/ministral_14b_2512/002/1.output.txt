### Strategy for Hex Policy

My strategy combines several key elements to create a strong Hex policy:

1. **Opening Moves**: Use a strong opening strategy (like the "optimal" opening moves) to gain early control.
2. **Pattern Recognition**: Look for immediate winning patterns or forced responses to opponent moves.
3. **Center Control**: Prioritize controlling the center of the board, which is crucial in Hex.
4. **Threat Detection**: Identify and block opponent threats that could lead to their victory.
5. **Connectivity**: Maximize the connectivity of your own stones to create multiple potential winning paths.
6. **Randomness**: Introduce controlled randomness to avoid predictable patterns.

The policy will:
- First check for immediate winning moves.
- Then check for opponent winning moves and block them.
- Use a strong opening strategy for the first few moves.
- For later moves, prioritize moves that maximize connectivity and control of the board.
- Use a simple evaluation function to score potential moves based on their strategic value.
