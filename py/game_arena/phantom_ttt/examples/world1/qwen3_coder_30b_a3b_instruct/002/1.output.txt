The strategy for this policy is to implement a smart approach for Phantom Tic Tac Toe that considers both the current board state and potential future outcomes, while avoiding actions that are already confirmed as yours. 

Key elements:
1. **Prioritize central and corner positions**: These are more strategically valuable in tic-tac-toe.
2. **Avoid confirmed moves**: If a cell is already marked as 1 (your confirmed move), don't try to place there.
3. **Consider potential wins**: Look for opportunities to form a line of 2 of your marks with one more move.
4. **Block opponent threats**: If opponent has two in a row, block it.
5. **Fallback to safe moves**: If no critical moves, pick a central or corner spot.

My implementation:
1. First checks if there's any immediate win or block opportunity.
2. Then avoids squares already confirmed as yours (board[i][j] == 1).
3. Prioritizes center (4), then corners (0,2,6,8), then edges (1,3,5,7).
4. Makes a move that's consistent with the game rules, always returning a legal move.

def check_win(board: List[List[int]]) -> bool:
