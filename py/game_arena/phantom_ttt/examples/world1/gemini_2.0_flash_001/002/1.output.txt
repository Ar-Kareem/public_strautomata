1.  **Prioritize Winning Moves:** Check if there's a move that immediately wins the game (creates three in a row). If so, play that move. Since we only have partial information, we must verify that playing the move will actually lead to a win by checking if all locations in the winning row/col/diag are >= 0 (not occupied by opponent).

2.  **Block Opponent's Winning Moves:** Check if the opponent is about to win (two in a row, potentially with a third possible move). If so, block their move. Because we do not have positive evidence of our opponent's moves, we can only choose to block a sequence where the other two cells are confirmed open for us to select using policy.

3.  **Play Center, Edges, then Corners:** If no winning or blocking move is available, play strategically by selecting the center cell first (if available), then the edges, then the corner cells. This follows a general Tic Tac Toe strategy.

4.  **Avoid Confirmed Cells:** When choosing moves, always prioritize cells where `board[row][col] == 0`. This avoids wasting moves on cells already known to be ours.

5.  **Random Legal Move:** If none of the above conditions are met, play a random valid legal move from list of `legal_moves`. This ensures that we always return a valid move.
