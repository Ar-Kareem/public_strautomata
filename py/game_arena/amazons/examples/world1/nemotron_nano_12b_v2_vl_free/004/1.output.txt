The solution must ensure that the move is legal, adhering to the game's rules. The policy should prioritize moves that maximize the opponent's mobility constraints.

### Approach
1. **Identify Valid Moves**: For each of your amazons, generate all possible queen-like moves. A valid move is a straight line (orthogonal or diagonal) to an empty square without any obstructions.
2. **Generate Arrow Shots**: After moving the amazon, determine all valid arrow shots in all 8 directions from the new position. The arrow must land on an empty square and its path must be unobstructed.
3. **Evaluate Candidates**: For each valid move and arrow, evaluate the candidate based on the length of the arrow path and the mobility of the amazon in its new position. The mobility is the number of queen moves available from the new position.
4. **Select Best Move**: Prioritize candidates with the longest arrow path and, in case of ties, the one with the highest mobility to ensure a strong offensive stance.

                for arrow_dr, arrow_dc in directions:

                    step_arrow = 1

                        ac = newc + arrow_dc * step_arrow

        from_c_shot = int(from_indices[1])

### Explanation
The `policy` function generates all valid moves for the current amazons, ensuring each move is followed by a legal arrow shot. It then selects the best move based on a heuristic that prioritizes the longest arrow length to maximize blocking effects, combined with the mobility of the amazon after moving. This balance of long-range blocking and flexibility ensures a strategic advantage in the game.
