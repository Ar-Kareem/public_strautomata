The strategy is to implement a simple yet effective move selection policy for Amazons on a 6x6 board. The key insight is that we want to make moves that both advance our position and limit our opponent's options. 

My approach:
1. First, I'll identify all of my amazons that can make legal moves
2. For each possible move, I'll compute a heuristic score evaluating:
   - How many squares I can potentially shoot arrows to from the landing position
   - Whether the move blocks opponent escape routes
   - Whether it maintains or improves the number of empty squares in my territory
3. I'll select the move with the highest heuristic score

For simplicity and to ensure correctness, I'll also use a slightly modified backtracking algorithm to validate moves and make sure they're legal.

I'll implement valid move generation for both amazon movement and arrow shooting, ensuring everything follows the 6x6 board constraints and the rule that paths must be unobstructed.
