The strategy for this policy is as follows:

1. **Evaluate Legal Moves**: First, the AI identifies all legal moves, which involve valid paths for the Amazon to move (like a queen in chess), followed by a valid arrow shot (also a queen-like path but with obstructions added) from the Amazon's destination square.

2. **Scoring System**: For each legal move, a score is computed using the following rubric:
   - **Immediate Threat**: A move is given higher priority if it directly threatens to restrict the opponent's movements.
   - **Board Control**: Moves that open up more space for the AI (i.e., end in a central area) are preferred.
   - **Offense/Defense Balance**: A balance is maintained between moves that eliminate future opponent moves and those that reinforce the AI's positions.

3. **Depth-First Search (DFS)**: The moves are prioritized to simulate a depth-first search to pick the most promising move rapidly. Since 1 second allows DFS without prunings in a 6x6 board, we do a step of quick evaluation and choose a top move.

4. **Fallback Logic**: If no offensive move is available, focus on defense by ensuring the AI's amazons stay positioned to avoid future blocking.
