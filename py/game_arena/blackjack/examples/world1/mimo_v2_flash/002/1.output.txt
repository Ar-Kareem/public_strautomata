The problem asks for a smart policy for a simplified Blackjack game against an opponent with a separate deck.

### Analysis

1.  **Game Dynamics**:
    *   **Opponent**: The opponent plays after we decide our strategy (or busts if they exceed the target). They likely play using a standard "hit until safe" strategy. The probability of the opponent busting increases significantly as their current hand value decreases.
    *   **Our Hand**: We have a deck of 13 cards. We know exactly what cards are left in our deck (initially all 13, removing cards as we draw).
    *   **Ace Handling**: Aces are dynamic. They are 11 if we are at or below the target, and 1 if we are above.

2.  **Strategy**:
    *   If we **stay**, our score is fixed (taking Aces as 11 if <= target, else 1). The opponent must beat this score without busting. If we are very close to `T`, the opponent has very little room to maneuver and likely busts.
    *   If we **hit**, we risk busting immediately, but we might improve our position.
    *   We need to estimate the probability of winning for each action.

3.  **Evaluation Function (Expected Win Probability)**:
    *   Let $P_{bust}$ be the probability the opponent busts.
    *   If we **stay**: 
        $$P_{win} = P_{bust} + (1 - P_{bust}) \times P(\text{Opponent Score} < \text{Our Score})$$
    *   If we **hit**: We must sum over all possible cards we can draw. For each outcome (new hand, potential bust, or new stay value), we compute the win probability against the opponent's distribution.
        $$P_{win} = \sum_{c \in Deck} \frac{1}{\text{cards\_left}} \times P_{outcome}(c)$$
        Where $P_{outcome}(c)$ is either 0 (if we bust) or the win probability of the new state (Stay or Hit again, which we recurse or approximate).

4.  **Opponent Simulation**:
    *   To calculate $P_{bust}$ and the distribution of opponent scores, I will simulate the opponent's decision process. The opponent hits if their safe score < Target.
    *   Since our deck and opponent's deck are independent and identical, we can use our deck's composition to estimate the opponent's deck probabilities (assuming uniform distribution of unseen cards). This is a standard assumption in card counting/Bayesian inference when the exact opponent deck is unknown but the initial set is known.

5.  **Algorithm**:
    *   Given `hand` and `target`.
    *   Calculate `current_value` (max safe value).
    *   **Branch 1 (Stay):** Calculate win rate against opponent.
    *   **Branch 2 (Hit):** Iterate through all unique card ranks in our remaining deck. Calculate the expected win rate if we draw that card.
    *   Return "HIT" if Expected Win Rate (Hit) > Expected Win Rate (Stay), else "STAY".
