To implement an effective Blackjack policy for a dynamic target $T$, we must calculate the expected value of "Hitting" versus "Staying."

1.  **Hand Value Calculation**: The value of "A" is flexible. It defaults to 11 unless that causes a bust, in which case it becomes 1.
2.  **Deck Tracking**: Since each player has their own 13-card deck, we can determine exactly which cards remain in the deck by subtracting the cards in our `hand` from the set of all 13 ranks.
3.  **Risk Analysis**:
    *   If we "STAY", our score is fixed.
    *   If we "HIT", we draw a card from the remaining pool. We calculate the probability of busting.
4.  **Policy Logic**:
    *   If our current value is already the `target`, we always "STAY".
    *   We calculate the "Success Rate" of hitting: the probability that the next card will not result in a bust.
    *   A simple but effective threshold for hitting in a competitive environment is to hit if the probability of not busting is greater than or equal to 50%, or if our current total is significantly lower than the target (e.g., less than $T - 4$), as staying on low numbers is almost a guaranteed loss against an opponent who might hit closer to the target.
