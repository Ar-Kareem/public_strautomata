### Understanding the Game Mechanics
1. **Movement Rules**: A piece moves horizontally, vertically, or diagonally by a number of squares equal to the total number of pieces (both players) in that line (row, column, or diagonal). For example, if there are 3 pieces in a row, the piece must move 3 squares in that row.
2. **Jumping**: Pieces can jump over friendly pieces but cannot jump over enemy pieces.
3. **Capturing**: A piece can land on and capture an enemy piece, removing it from the board.
4. **Objective**: Connect all your pieces into a single contiguous group (8-directionally connected).

### Policy Design
To create a strong policy, I need to consider:
1. **Legal Moves**: First, generate all legal moves from the current board state.
2. **Evaluation Function**: Score each legal move based on how it contributes to the goal of connecting all my pieces. This could involve:
   - Increasing the number of connected components (but we want to reduce this to 1).
   - Capturing opponent pieces (since fewer opponent pieces mean less obstruction).
   - Moving towards the center or strategic positions.
   - Ensuring that the move doesn't leave my pieces in a vulnerable position.
3. **Heuristics**: Use heuristics to prioritize moves that:
   - Reduce the number of connected components of my pieces.
   - Capture opponent pieces.
   - Move towards the center or other strategic positions.
   - Avoid moves that leave my pieces isolated or vulnerable.

### Implementation Steps
1. **Generate Legal Moves**: For each of my pieces, determine all possible moves based on the rules.
   - For each direction (8 possible), calculate the number of pieces in that line (including the piece itself).
   - The piece must move exactly that many squares in that direction.
   - Check if the path is clear (no enemy pieces to jump over).
   - Ensure the landing position is valid (within bounds, not occupied by a friendly piece unless jumping over it).
2. **Evaluate Moves**: For each legal move, evaluate how it affects the board state.
   - After making the move, check if the number of connected components of my pieces decreases.
   - Check if any opponent pieces are captured.
   - Consider the position of the moved piece (e.g., central positions are often better).
3. **Select Best Move**: Choose the move with the highest evaluation score.

### Optimizations

- Precompute the number of pieces in each line (row, column, diagonal) to speed up legal move generation.

### Example Evaluation Function
- **Connectivity Bonus**: If the move reduces the number of connected components, give a high score.
- **Capture Bonus**: If the move captures an opponent piece, give a high score.
- **Central Control**: Prefer moves that bring pieces closer to the center.
- **Threat Reduction**: Avoid moves that leave my pieces vulnerable to future captures.
