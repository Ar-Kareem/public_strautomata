The strategy for this policy is a hybrid of targeted hunting and probabilistic search, optimized for efficiency and speed:

1. **Initial Phase (Sparse Hits)**: When few or no hits have been registered, use a checkerboard pattern to efficiently cover the board. This ensures we hit potential ship segments with minimal redundancy, since ships of length 2+ must span at least two adjacent cells.

2. **Targeting Phase (After First Hit)**: Once a hit is found, trigger a "hunt-and-destroy" mode:
   - Explore the 4 orthogonal neighbors of the hit in priority order (up, right, down, left).
   - If a second hit is found in line with the first, determine the ship's orientation and fire along that line to sink the ship as quickly as possible.
   - If multiple hits are found on the same ship, continue targeting along the line with logical predictability (e.g., extend in both directions).

3. **Probability Masking**: For unknown cells, compute a probability map based on ship placement constraints. For each unknown cell, calculate how many possible ship placements (of remaining unsunk ships) can cover it. Fire at the cell with the highest probability.

4. **Optimization**:
   - Track which ships are still alive (by comparing cumulative hits to known ship lengths: 5,4,3,3,2).
   - Use dynamic pruning: if a ship of length L cannot fit in any orientation in a region, mark those cells as impossible.
   - Caching of previous computations to stay within 1s time limit.

5. **Fallback**: If no clear target exists (rare), revert to checkerboard in uncovered regions.

This strategy ensures both rapid initial coverage and efficient target prioritization after hits, maximizing the chance of sinking ships before the opponent.
