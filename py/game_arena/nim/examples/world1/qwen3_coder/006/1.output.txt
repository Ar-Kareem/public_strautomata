The strategy for this policy is based on the theory of misère Nim. In misère Nim, the goal is to avoid taking the last object. The key principle is to aim for a position where the XOR (nim-sum) of all pile sizes equals zero when there are multiple piles with more than one object. If we are already in such a P-position (losing position), we try to leave an odd number of single-object piles for the opponent.

The algorithm works as follows:
1. First, we calculate the XOR of all pile sizes.
2. If the XOR is non-zero (N-position, winning position), we look for a move that makes the XOR zero by reducing one pile appropriately. We prioritize moves that maintain multiple non-empty piles, avoiding moves that leave only single-object piles if possible.
3. If the XOR is zero (P-position, losing position), we're forced to make a move that leaves the opponent in a winning position. In this case, we remove one object from the largest pile to minimize the advantage we're giving away.
4. Edge cases are handled to ensure we always make a legal move.

This strategy is mathematically sound for misère Nim and should perform well against most heuristic or random players.

    xor_sum = 0
    for pile in piles:
        xor_sum ^= pile

    multi_object_piles = sum(1 for pile in piles if pile > 1)
