The strategy for this policy is based on the winning conditions of the Nim game. In Nim, a position can be characterized by the "Nim-sum," which is the bitwise XOR of the sizes of all piles. The key points of this strategy are:

1. If the Nim-sum is zero, the position is losing for the player to move, meaning they cannot force a win with perfect play from the opponent.
2. If the Nim-sum is non-zero, there exists at least one move that can turn the position into a losing one for the opponent. The player should aim to create a position where the Nim-sum becomes zero on their opponent's turn.

The policy will always check the Nim-sum. If it is greater than zero, the player will attempt to take stones from one of the piles to make the Nim-sum zero. If it is already zero, the player will take stones from any pile legally, as any move will lead them into a losing position if the opponent plays optimally.

Here’s the implementation of the policy:

```python
def policy(piles: list[int]) -> str:
    nim_sum = piles[0] ^ piles[1] ^ piles[2] ^ piles[3]
    
    # Try to find a move that makes the new nim_sum zero
    for i in range(4):
        if piles[i] > 0:  # Only consider non-empty piles
            new_pile_count = piles[i] ^ nim_sum
            if new_pile_count < piles[i]:  # Valid move found
                return f'{i},{piles[i] - new_pile_count}'
    
    # If no move makes the nim sum zero, take from any pile legally
    for i in range(4):
        if piles[i] > 0:  
            return f'{i},1'  # Take 1 item from the first non-empty pile
```
