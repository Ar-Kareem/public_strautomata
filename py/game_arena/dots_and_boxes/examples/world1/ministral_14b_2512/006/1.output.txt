### Strategy for this Policy

The strategy for this policy is based on a **combination of aggressive and defensive play** with the following key principles:

1. **Prioritize Completing Boxes**: Always try to draw an edge that completes one or more of your boxes (i.e., an edge with exactly three sides already drawn). This maximizes your immediate score and forces the opponent to respond without capturing boxes.

2. **Block Opponent's Potential Boxes**: If the opponent has an edge that would complete three sides of their box (i.e., a "threat"), block it by drawing the fourth edge. This prevents them from scoring in their next move.

3. **Avoid Giving Free Boxes to Opponent**: Never draw an edge that leaves exactly three sides of a box open for the opponent (unless it's unavoidable). This ensures they cannot capture a box on their next move.

4. **Center Control**: Prefer edges in the center of the grid (rows/columns 1-3) to maintain control and limit the opponent's opportunities to complete boxes. This is especially important in early-game scenarios.

5. **Corner and Edge Focus**: In later stages, prioritize edges that are adjacent to corners or edges of the grid, as these often lead to more box completions.

6. **Randomness for Edge Cases**: If no obvious aggressive or defensive moves are available, choose a random legal edge to avoid predictable patterns. This adds unpredictability to the policy.

7. **Turn-Based Adaptation**: Adjust the strategy based on whether the last move captured a box (i.e., if you just took another turn). If so, prioritize further aggressive moves; otherwise, balance aggression and defense.

### Implementation Notes
- The policy uses **numpy arrays** for efficient state representation and computation.
- It checks for **legal moves** by verifying if an edge is empty (`0` in `horizontal` or `vertical`).
- It evaluates **potential box completions** by counting the number of edges around each box.
- The **move selection** is deterministic (no randomness in the core logic) but can be extended with randomness if needed for edge cases.
