### Strategy for this Policy

The strategy for this policy is based on a **modified greedy algorithm with lookahead and box completion prioritization**. Here's the breakdown:

1. **Box Completion Priority**: First, the policy looks for moves that complete **at least one box** for the current player. This is crucial because capturing boxes gives an extra turn, and the player who captures more boxes wins.
   - If multiple such moves exist, it prefers moves that complete **more than one box** (e.g., a move that closes two adjacent boxes).
   - If no such moves exist, it defaults to a greedy approach.

2. **Greedy Lookahead for Opponent's Boxes**:
   - If no immediate box-completing moves are available, the policy avoids moves that would allow the opponent to complete a box on their next turn (i.e., moves that leave exactly **three sides of a box** for the opponent).
   - It prioritizes moves that **block opponent's potential box completions** (e.g., moves that leave the opponent with no possible box completions in the next turn).

3. **Greedy Edge Selection**:
   - If no box-completing or blocking moves are available, the policy selects the **edge that is part of the most potential boxes** (i.e., the edge that contributes to the most unclosed boxes).
   - This is done to maximize future opportunities for capturing boxes.

4. **Fallback to Random Legal Move**:
   - If the board is nearly full and no strategic moves are left, the policy picks a **random legal move** to avoid getting stuck in a non-optimal pattern.

5. **Edge Cases**:
   - If the opponent has no possible moves (all edges are drawn), the policy will still return a legal move (though this shouldn't happen in a valid game).
   - The policy ensures that it **never completes a box for the opponent** unless it cannot avoid it (i.e., it doesn't make moves that leave exactly three sides for the opponent unless forced).

6. **Efficiency**:
   - The policy uses **bitwise operations and precomputed masks** to quickly evaluate the state of the board and determine which boxes are completable or which edges are part of the most potential boxes.
   - It avoids unnecessary computations by focusing only on the edges that are legal (i.e., `horizontal` or `vertical` values are `0`).

def _select_move_by_potential(moves: List[Tuple[int, int, str]], horizontal: np.ndarray, vertical: np.ndarray, capture: np.ndarray) -> str:

### Explanation of Key Functions:
1. **`policy`**:
   - Collects all legal moves (`horizontal` or `vertical` edges with value `0`).
   - Prioritizes moves that complete **one or more boxes** (either single or multi-box moves).

This strategy is designed to be **aggressive in capturing boxes** while **defensive against opponent's threats**, making it strong in the Dots and Boxes arena.
