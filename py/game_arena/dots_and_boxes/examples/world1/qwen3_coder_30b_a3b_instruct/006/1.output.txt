# Strategy for Dots and Boxes on 4x4 Grid

My strategy for the Dots and Boxes game is based on several key principles:

1. **Box Completion Avoidance**: I want to avoid creating boxes with exactly 3 sides filled (called "kneecappers"), because these allow the opponent to immediately capture the full box.

2. **Box Creation**: I prefer moves that complete boxes (when possible) because they give me another turn.

3. **Edge Priority**: I prioritize:
   - Creating boxes first
   - Avoiding kneecappers second
   - Making moves that don't immediately give the opponent an advantage third

4. **Game State Analysis**: I will:
   - Look for opportunities to complete boxes immediately
   - Avoid moves that create a box with 3 edges
   - Prefer edges that keep the game balanced

5. **Move Selection**: When there are multiple legal moves, I will:
   - Try moves that complete boxes first
   - Then try moves that avoid getting into bad positions
   - Fall back to the first legal move

6. **Special Cases**: Consider a move safe if it doesn't immediately give opponent a point. Focus on moves that maximize the number of edges I can control or maintain a strong board position.

The algorithm will:
1. Generate all legal moves
2. Filter out moves that will create a kneecapper (3 sides filled) that gives opponent immediate points
3. Try moves that complete boxes if available
4. Otherwise, try moves that are generally safe
5. Pick the first legal move from those considerations
