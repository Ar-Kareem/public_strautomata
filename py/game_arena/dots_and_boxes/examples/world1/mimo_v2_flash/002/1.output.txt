To compete in the Dots and Boxes arena on a 4x4 grid, I need a policy that balances immediate scoring with strategic positioning to avoid giving the opponent free points.

### Strategy Analysis
1.  **Capture Opportunity**: The highest priority is completing boxes. If a move completes one or more boxes, I score points and get an extra turn. This is a massive advantage.
2.  **Avoidance of "Dangers"**: The worst scenario is drawing an edge that leaves a box with exactly 3 sides filled (a "double-cross" or "trap" scenario). This hands the opponent a guaranteed capture and another turn.
3.  **The "Long Chain" Trap**: In a 4x4 grid, the center often forms a long chain. The standard winning strategy is to leave the long chain for last. If I can force a situation where I take the boxes *before* the chain is opened, I win.
4.  **Current State Analysis**:
    *   I will scan the board for valid moves.
    *   I will categorize moves based on the number of sides they complete.
    *   I will specifically look for moves that create a "double-cross" (giving the opponent 2+ boxes or a free turn).
    *   If no immediate capture is available, I will prioritize moves that keep the number of sides per box strictly under 3.

### Implementation Plan
1.  **Count Neighbors**: For every potential move, count how many neighbors (adjacent edges) are already drawn.
2.  **Simulate Completion**: Calculate how many boxes would be closed by a move.
    *   If `neighbor_count == 3` for a box, playing there completes it (score +1).
    *   If `neighbor_count == 2` for a box, playing there creates a dangerous 3-sided box (should be avoided unless no other moves exist).
3.  **Select Move**:
    *   **Priority 1**: Moves that close boxes (especially multiple boxes).
    *   **Priority 2**: Moves that leave neighbors < 2 (safe moves).
    *   **Priority 3**: Moves that leave neighbors == 2 (risky, but necessary to progress).
    *   **Priority 4**: Moves that leave neighbors == 3 (desperate, only if the board is full).
