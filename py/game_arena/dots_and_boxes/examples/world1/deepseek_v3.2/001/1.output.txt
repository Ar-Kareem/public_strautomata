**Thinking process**  
I’ll design a policy for Dots and Boxes on a 4×4 box grid that:  

1. **First, look for immediate captures** – if there’s a box with three edges filled, take it.  
2. **Avoid giving opponent easy captures** – don’t create a box with three sides filled unless it’s forced or part of a good chain strategy.  
3. **Count chains and use double-dealing strategy** – in Dots and Boxes, if you can force a long chain of boxes to be opened by you, you can take all but one and hand the last to opponent while keeping the rest.  
4. **Prioritize safe moves** – if no three-edge boxes exist, choose a move that doesn’t create a three-edge box for opponent, preferring edges that border as few open boxes as possible.  
5. **Fallback** – if all else fails, pick a random legal move.  

Given the 4×4 grid, I can implement a simple heuristic:  
- Identify all moves that give away a capture immediately (bad).  
- Identify all moves that capture boxes immediately (good).  
- For neutral moves, pick those that minimize the number of new boxes with three edges filled.  

Also, near the endgame (most boxes captured), we can simulate a few steps ahead with a minimax of depth 2 or 3, but within 1 second.  

I’ll implement:  
1. Function to list legal moves.  
2. Function to find capturing moves.  
3. Function to evaluate how many 3-edge boxes a move creates for opponent next turn.  
4. If no capture, choose move minimizing opponent’s possible captures next turn.
