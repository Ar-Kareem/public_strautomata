I'll create a smart Dots and Boxes policy that considers both immediate gains and long-term strategy.

My approach will be:
1. First priority: Complete boxes when possible (free points)
2. Second priority: Avoid creating boxes with 3 sides (giving opponent free points)  
3. Third priority: Control the center and create flexible positions
4. Fallback: Make any legal move

Let me think through the strategy:
- If I can complete a box, I should always do it
- If an opponent is about to complete a box, I should block it if possible
- Avoid moves that create 3-sided boxes for the opponent
- Prefer moves that keep options open rather than boxing myself in
