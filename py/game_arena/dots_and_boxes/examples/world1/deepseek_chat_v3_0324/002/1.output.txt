Let me outline the logic for this Dots and Boxes policy:

1. **Priority System**: The policy follows a hierarchical decision-making process:
   - First look for moves that complete a box (must-capture moves)
   - Then look for safe moves that don't create 3-side boxes for opponent
   - Then look for moves that create chains or potential double-cross opportunities
   - Finally fall back to random legal moves

2. **Defensive Play**: The policy avoids creating 3-side boxes for the opponent whenever possible by:
   - Identifying potential dangerous moves
   - Prioritizing moves that minimize opponent's opportunities

3. **Offensive Strategy**: When no immediate captures are available:
   - Creates potential double-cross situations where possible
   - Builds chains that can lead to multiple captures in future turns

4. **Edge Cases**: Handles all edge cases to ensure only legal moves are returned
