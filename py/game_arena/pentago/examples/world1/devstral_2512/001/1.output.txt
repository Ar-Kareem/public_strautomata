To implement a move-selection policy for Pentago, I need to consider the following steps:

1. **Understand the Game Rules**: The game involves placing a marble and then rotating a quadrant. The goal is to create a line of 5 marbles (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) while preventing the opponent from doing the same.

2. **Legal Move Generation**: The policy must generate all possible legal moves. A legal move consists of:
   - Placing a marble in an empty cell.
   - Rotating one of the four quadrants either left (L) or right (R).

3. **Move Evaluation**: For each legal move, simulate the placement and rotation, then evaluate the resulting board state to determine if it leads to a win or a better position.

4. **Heuristic for Move Selection**: Since a full game tree search might be too slow, a heuristic can be used to evaluate the board state. Possible heuristics include:
   - **Immediate Win**: Check if the move results in a 5-in-a-row for the player.
   - **Block Opponent**: Check if the move blocks the opponent from getting a 5-in-a-row.
   - **Center Control**: Prefer moves that control the center of the board or quadrants.
   - **Randomness**: If no immediate win or block is found, choose a random legal move to ensure the policy always returns a legal move.

5. **Implementation Steps**:
   - **Generate Legal Moves**: Iterate over all empty cells and all possible quadrant rotations.
   - **Simulate Moves**: For each move, simulate the placement and rotation to get the new board state.
   - **Check for Win**: After simulation, check if the player has a 5-in-a-row.
   - **Check for Block**: Similarly, check if the opponent would have a 5-in-a-row if they were to move next.
   - **Select Best Move**: Prioritize moves that lead to an immediate win, then moves that block the opponent, and finally random moves.
