**Pentago Strategy Analysis**

To win at Pentago, the immediate priority is to detect and execute a "winning move." A winning move involves placing a marble to create a 5-in-a-row and then rotating a quadrant to finalize the alignment, or rotating a quadrant to unblock a previously placed marble to form a 5-in-a-row.

Secondary priorities are blocking the opponent's winning moves and preventing their forks (setups for multiple winning threats). I will implement a brute-force search to evaluate every possible move (cell + rotation) and select the best one based on a hierarchy of objectives:
1.  **Self-Win:** Maximize own 5-in-a-row probability.
2.  **Opponent Block:** Minimize opponent's 5-in-a-row probability.
3.  **Strategic Scoring:** Maximize own 4-in-a-row and 3-in-a-row counts.
4.  **Center Control:** Favor placing marbles in the center and quadrants 0 and 3 for better connectivity.

I will use numpy for efficient board representation and simulation.
