My strategy for this policy is a brute-force search with a specialized evaluation function. Since the Pentago board is relatively small (36 cells) and the game is deterministic with perfect information, a minimax approach is ideal. However, standard minimax is too slow if we consider the full game tree.

Therefore, I will implement the following:

1.  **Heuristic Evaluation**: I will use a lookup table (or weighted sum) to evaluate the board state. This function will heavily prioritize creating lines of 4 or 5 marbles (winning threats) and blocking the opponent's similar threats. It will also value "open" lines that can be extended.
2.  **Move Generation**: I will generate all valid moves (placing a piece and rotating a quadrant).
3.  **Search Algorithm**: I will use a **Negamax** algorithm (a simplified version of Minimax) with **Alpha-Beta pruning**. To ensure the move is calculated within the 1-second limit, I will limit the search depth to a reasonable number (e.g., 3 or 4) which allows exploring immediate threats and responses.
4.  **Win Detection**: The search will prioritize paths that lead to an immediate win for our side.

This approach balances speed and intelligence, allowing the AI to see a few moves ahead and react to immediate threats while maintaining a solid positional strategy.
