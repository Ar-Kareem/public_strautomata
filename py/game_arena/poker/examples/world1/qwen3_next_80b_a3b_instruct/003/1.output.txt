The strategy for this policy is to evaluate the strength of the hole card in context with the community cards (board) and the current betting situation. Since this is a single-hole-card Hold'em variant (unusual but permitted by the state), we must rely on the single card's rank and potential to make strong hands (pair, top pair, sets, draws, etc.). 

We implement a multi-layered decision policy:
1. Preflop (no public cards): Play aggressively with high cards (A, K, Q, J, 10), fold low cards (< 7), call mid-range (7-9), and raise with premium cards (A, K, Q).
2. Postflop: Evaluate the board for possible pairs, flush draws, straight draws. Compare your card’s rank to the highest board card. If you have top pair or better, bet aggressively. If you have a draw and pot odds are favorable, call or raise. If you're behind and the opponent is betting heavily, fold unless you have a strong draw or backdoor equity.
3. Use pot odds to justify calls: If the chance of winning (estimating based on outs) times the pot is greater than the cost to call, call or raise.
4. Use all-in when extremely strong (e.g., top pair with high kicker on coordinated board, or when opponent is likely bluffing).
5. Adjust for opponent aggression: If the opponent is betting large, they may be bluffing or have a strong hand — we weigh this by considering equity and stack depth.

We use approximate equity calculations based on outs and the number of unseen cards. Since we have only one hole card, calculating combos is simplified.
