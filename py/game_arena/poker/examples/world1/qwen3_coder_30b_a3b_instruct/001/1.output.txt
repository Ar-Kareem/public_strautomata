# Strategy

My strategy for this poker policy is to create a sophisticated hand strength evaluation system combined with game theory optimal (GTO) bluffing and pot odds considerations. Here's my approach:

1. **Hand Strength Evaluation**: I'll calculate the probability of having the best hand based on my hole cards and public cards. Since we're dealing with only one hole card, it's more focused on position and potential combinations rather than full hand strength.

2. **Pot Odds and Expected Value**: I'll consider the pot odds when deciding whether to call or raise. When the pot odds are favorable, I'll be more aggressive; when not, I'll play tighter.

3. **Game Theory Optimal Bluffing**: I'll include a bluffing component that considers the opponent's betting patterns and pot size. When I have a weak hand or marginal hand and the pot is large enough, I'll occasionally raise or bluff.

4. **Positional Awareness**: Though not directly available from the state, I'll adjust strategy based on whether I'm the first or second player based on pot size and betting dynamics.

5. **Stack Size and Tournament Style**: I'll be aggressive with larger stacks and more conservative when at risk.

6. **Adjusting for Opponent Behavior**: If the opponent is playing tight and checking frequently, I'll try to steal pots with raises. If they're raising often, I'll play more defensively.

7. **Simple but Effective Logic Flow**:
   - If I can win without a bet (check/call), do that
   - If I have a good hand or strong potential (high card, suited, possible straight/flush draws), raise or all-in
   - If hand strength looks poor but pot odds are good, call
   - If hand strength is poor and bet is excessive, fold
   - Incorporate bluffing when pot is large enough to make it profitable

The implementation will focus on a probabilistic approach to evaluating hand strength using the number of outs for drawing hands, and use the game state parameters to make strategic decisions.
