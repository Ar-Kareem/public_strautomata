To tackle the challenge of creating a robust and effective policy for a hold'em poker game, we implement a hand strength evaluator and a Monte Carlo simulation-based equity estimator. This approach enables us to assess the strength of our hand relative to all possible opponent hands and simulated board cards, allowing informed decisions on whether to fold, call, raise, or go all-in.

---

### 🧠 Strategy Overview

1. **Monte Carlo Simulation**: We simulate multiple possible outcomes based on:
   - Opponent's hole cards (randomly sampled from the remaining deck).
   - Remaining community cards (turn and river, if needed).
   - Full 7-card evaluation for both players.

2. **Hand Evaluation**: We simulate all possible 5-card combinations from the 7 available cards (our 2 hole cards + 5 community cards) and evaluate the best hand.

3. **Decision Logic**:
   - **Fold**: If our estimated equity is low and the cost to call is high.
   - **Call**: If equity is moderate and the pot odds are favorable.
   - **Raise/All-in**: If equity is high and we can place significant pressure on the opponent.

---

### 🧠 Key Components

#### ✅ Hand Evaluation Function
This function evaluates the strength of a 5-card poker hand, supporting standard hand rankings like flush, straight, pair, etc.

#### ✅ Monte Carlo Simulation
We simulate 1000 possible opponent hands and board outcomes to estimate equity.

### 🧠 Strategy Summary

- **Fold** early in pre-flop if hand is too weak (e.g., equity < 35%).
- **Call** if equity is moderate and pot odds are favorable.
- **Raise/All-in** if equity is strong and we can increase the pressure on the opponent.
