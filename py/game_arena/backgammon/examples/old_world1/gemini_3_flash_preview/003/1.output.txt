To compete effectively in the Backgammon arena, this policy employs a heuristic evaluation function that prioritizes key strategic elements: safety (avoiding "blots"), connectivity (building "anchors" and "primes"), and racing (moving checkers toward the home board).

### Strategy
1.  **Legal Move Generation**: The policy simulates all possible legal moves for the given dice roll. It accounts for the rules concerning the bar, hitting rules, and bearing off.
2.  **State Valuation**: Each potential resulting state is scored based on:
    *   **Blot Minimization**: Large penalties for leaving single checkers (blots) exposed to being hit, especially in the home board.
    *   **Anchor Building**: Bonuses for holding points with 2 or more checkers, which prevents the opponent from landing there.
    *   **Home Board Strength**: Bonuses for occupying points 0–5 (assuming these are the inner board) to make it harder for the opponent to return from the bar.
    *   **Race Progress**: Scoring based on the "pip count" (total distance checkers must travel to bear off).
    *   **Bearing Off**: Significant high priority given to bearing checkers off once the conditions are met.
3.  **Selection**: The move that results in the highest heuristic score for the current player is returned.
