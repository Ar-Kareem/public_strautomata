The strategy for this policy is:

The AI will prioritize moves that improve its position and hinder the opponent. This involves:

1.  **Moving off the bar:** If checkers are on the bar, the AI will prioritize moving them back into the game.
2.  **Hitting opponent's blots:** The AI will hit opponent's single checkers (blots) whenever possible to send them to the bar.
3.  **Making points:** The AI will try to create blocked points (points with two or more checkers) to restrict the opponent's movement. Especially blocking the opponent's homeboard exit.
4. **Bearing off:** As soon as possible, the AI will bear off checkers from its home board.
5. **Avoiding leaving blots:** The AI will minimize leaving single checkers exposed to being hit.
6. **Prioritize Higher Die first:** If possible

This policy is implemented using a series of checks to determine valid moves and selecting the first valid move found based on the priority list above. The code ensures that only legal moves are returned, even if the optimal move cannot be determined within the time limit. The move selection considers the dice values and prioritizes playing both dice when possible.
