### **Strategy for this Backgammon Policy**
This policy aims to balance **aggressive play** (attacking the opponent's checkers) and **defensive play** (protecting my own checkers) while ensuring **legal compliance** (always returning a valid move). The decision-making process is structured as follows:

1. **Bar Priority**: If I have checkers on the bar (`my_bar > 0`), I always move them first (since the engine enforces this rule).
2. **Bearing Off**: If all my checkers are in my home board (`A0..A5`), I prioritize bearing off (removing checkers from the board) to maximize my score.
3. **Attacking Opponent**: If the opponent has vulnerable checkers (1 or 2 checkers on a point), I try to **hit them** (land on their point) to force them into my home board.
4. **Building Prime**: If my checkers are in a **prime (6+ consecutive points)**, I avoid breaking it unless necessary to make a legal move or to attack.
5. **Doubling Dice**: If both dice can be legally played, I prefer moves that **double the dice** (e.g., moving both checkers to the same point) to create strong positions.
6. **High Die First**: If only one die can be played, I always use the **higher die** (as per legal rules).
7. **Safe Moves**: If no aggressive or defensive moves are available, I make the **safest possible move** (e.g., moving checkers toward my home board or avoiding opponent threats).

The policy uses **static evaluation** (predefined weights for positions) and **heuristics** to decide moves quickly within the 1-second constraint. It does not perform deep lookahead but instead relies on **positional strength** and **immediate threats**.
