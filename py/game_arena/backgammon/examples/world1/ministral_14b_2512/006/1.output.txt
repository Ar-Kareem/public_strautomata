### **Backgammon Policy Strategy: "Aggressive Home Board Control with Bar Priority"**

This policy aims to **maximize offensive opportunities** while **minimizing risk** in the opponent's home board. It follows these key principles:

1. **Bar Priority**: If any of my checkers are on the bar (`my_bar > 0`), I will always move them first (using the lower die if possible, otherwise the higher die).
2. **Higher Die First**: When moving from the bar or my own home board (A0–A5), I prioritize the **higher die** to avoid leaving vulnerable checkers.
3. **Opponent’s Home Board Attacks**:
   - If I can **hit or re-hit** the opponent’s checkers in their home board (A0–A5), I will do so **aggressively** (using the higher die first).
   - If the opponent has **blots (single checkers)** in their home board, I will target them to force them into my outer board.
4. **Safe Moves in My Home Board**:
   - If all my checkers are in my home board (A0–A5), I will **bear off** (move checkers to the off-table) using the higher die first.
5. **Middle Board Positioning**:
   - If I cannot attack the opponent’s home board, I will **avoid leaving checkers in vulnerable positions** (e.g., not moving into points with 2+ opponent checkers).
   - I will **prefer moving checkers forward** (toward A0–A5) to build a strong anchor.
6. **Doubling Cube Consideration** (if available in future extensions):
   - If the opponent has doubled, I will **take the cube** if the position is favorable.
   - If I have doubled, I will **avoid risky moves** that could allow the opponent to redouble.

### **Key Features of the Policy**
1. **Bar Handling**: Always moves from the bar first, avoiding opponent-heavy points.
2. **Home Board Attacks**: Prioritizes hitting opponent blots in their home board (A0–A5).
3. **Safe Forward Movement**: Prefers moving checkers toward the opponent’s home board (A0–A5) to build an anchor.
4. **Bearing Off**: If all checkers are in my home board, it bears them off optimally.
5. **Legal-Move Compliance**: Strictly follows the engine’s constraints (higher die first, no moving into 2+ opponent checkers, etc.).
