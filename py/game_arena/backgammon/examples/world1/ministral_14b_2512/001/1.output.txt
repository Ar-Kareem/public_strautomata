### **Strategy for My Backgammon Policy**
My policy is designed to be **aggressive yet strategic**, balancing **offensive and defensive play** while respecting the rules of the game. The key principles are:

1. **Bar Priority**: If I have checkers on the bar (`my_bar > 0`), I will always move them first (using the lower die if possible, but prioritizing legal re-entry).
2. **Blot Avoidance**: I will avoid moving onto points occupied by **2+ opponent checkers** (engine enforces this, but I optimize to minimize exposure).
3. **Doubling Down**: If both dice can be legally played, I will **always use both** (engine enforces this, but I ensure maximum efficiency).
4. **Higher Die First**: If only one die can be played, I will **always use the higher die** (engine enforces this, but I confirm no forced lower-die moves).
5. **Bearing Off**: When all my checkers are in my home board (`A0..A5`), I will **prioritize bearing off** (removing checkers from the board) while maintaining a safe position.
6. **Attacking Opponent’s Blots**: I will target **opponent blots (single checkers)** to force them into vulnerable positions.
7. **Building Prime**: I will try to **build a strong prime (block)** near the opponent’s home board to trap their checkers.
8. **Avoiding Weak Anchors**: I will avoid creating **exposed checkers** (e.g., leaving a single checker deep in the opponent’s territory).
9. **Doubling Cube Consideration**: If the game involves a doubling cube (not in the given state but could be extended), I would **accept doubles aggressively** when ahead and **pass when behind** to maximize risk-reward.
10. **Randomness for Uncertainty**: In ambiguous positions (e.g., no clear blot or prime), I will introduce **controlled randomness** to avoid predictable play.

The policy uses **static evaluation** (based on board position) and **heuristics** (e.g., blot targeting, prime building) to decide moves. It avoids complex Monte Carlo simulations due to the **1-second constraint** but remains strong by leveraging **positional awareness** and **rule-based optimizations**.

### **Key Features of the Implementation**
1. **Bearing Off**: If all checkers are in the home board (`A0..A5`), it greedily removes them (prioritizing higher dice).
2. **Bar Handling**: Always moves checkers from the bar first, preferring re-entry with the lower die if legal.
3. **Blot Targeting**: Prioritizes moves that hit opponent’s single checkers (blots) to force them into vulnerable positions.
4. **Prime Building**: Attempts to create a strong prime (block) near the opponent’s home board (`0..5`).
5. **Blot Avoidance**: Avoids leaving single checkers deep in the opponent’s territory (`0..12`).
6. **Legal Move Enforcement**: Always returns a valid move (or `P,P` if none are possible) while adhering to the rules.
7. **Efficiency**: Uses **O(24) per die** checks, ensuring it runs within the **1-second constraint**.
