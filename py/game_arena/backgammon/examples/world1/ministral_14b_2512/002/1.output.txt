### **Backgammon Policy Strategy: "Aggressive Home Board Control with Bar Priority"**

The strategy for this policy is designed to **maximize positional advantage** while adhering to legal constraints. Here’s the breakdown:

1. **Bar Priority**:
   - If any checkers are on the bar (`my_bar > 0`), the policy will always move them first (using the lower die first if possible, but respecting the "play higher die first" rule when only one die is valid).
   - The goal is to **enter checkers safely** (avoiding points with 2+ opposing checkers) while minimizing exposure to blots.

2. **Home Board Dominance**:
   - If no checkers are on the bar, the policy **prioritizes moving checkers into the home board (A0–A5)** to build a strong anchor.
   - It avoids leaving blots (single checkers) in the opponent’s home board unless necessary for a forced move.

3. **Doubling Cube Consideration (if applicable)**:
   - While the engine does not provide cube information, the policy implicitly assumes that **aggressive play is rewarded** (e.g., forcing the opponent into weak positions).
   - If bearing off is possible (`my_off < 15` and all checkers are in the home board), the policy will **prioritize scoring** while maintaining safe positions.

4. **Avoiding Weak Positions**:
   - The policy **prefers moving checkers forward** (toward the opponent’s home board) rather than backward (toward the bar) to maintain momentum.
   - If a die cannot be legally played (due to opponent’s checkers), the policy will **pass** (`H:P,P` or `L:P,P`).

5. **Blot Handling**:
   - If a blot (single opposing checker) is on a point where the policy can **hit and run** (e.g., moving a checker to a safe point after hitting), it will do so.
   - Otherwise, it **avoids hitting blots** unless the move is forced.

6. **Bearing Off**:
   - If all checkers are in the home board (`my_pts[0:6] == [sum(my_pts[0:6]), 0, 0, 0, 0, 0]`), the policy will **score checkers** in descending order (highest points first) while respecting the dice.

### **Key Features of the Policy**
1. **Bar Handling**:
   - Always moves bar checkers first, entering from the **highest possible point** (to minimize exposure).
   - Uses the **lower die first** (unless only one die is valid, then plays higher die first).

2. **Home Board Focus**:
   - Prefer moving checkers **into the home board (A0–A5)** to build a strong anchor.
   - Avoids leaving blots in the opponent’s home board unless forced.

3. **Bearing Off**:
   - Scores checkers **from highest to lowest** when bearing off is possible.

4. **Legal Move Enforcement**:
   - Respects the "play higher die first" rule when only one die is valid.
   - Always plays both dice if possible.
   - Passes (`P`) if no legal moves are available.

5. **Efficiency**:
   - Uses **numpy arrays** for fast computations.
   - Prioritizes **safe moves** (avoiding opponent’s 2+ checkers).
