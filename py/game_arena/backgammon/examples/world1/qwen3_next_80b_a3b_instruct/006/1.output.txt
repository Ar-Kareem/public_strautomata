The strategy for this policy is to implement a lookahead-based heuristic that prioritizes safety, efficiency, and bearing-off potential while respecting all game rules. The approach is:

1. **Bar Priority**: If checkers are on the bar, must re-enter first. Try to re-enter on points with 0 or 1 opponent checker (safe points), preferring points closer to home.
2. **Bearing Off Optimization**: If all checkers are in home board (points 0-5), prioritize bearing off checkers from the farthest points first (to clear the board efficiently).
3. **Point Control**: Avoid leaving blots (single checkers) that can be hit. Prefer building blocks (2+ checkers).
4. **Dice Utilization**: Use both dice when possible. If forced to use one, choose the higher die for maximum progress.
5. **Forward Progress**: When not in home board, move checkers toward home (lower indices) with preference for moves that land on safe points.
6. **Lookahead Evaluation**: For each legal sequence of moves, compute a score based on: 
   - Number of checkers borne off (favor this)
   - Number of checkers remaining on the board (favor less)
   - Number of blots (penalize)
   - Distance of checkers from home (favor closer)
   - Control of key points (e.g., 5-point, 6-point)
7. **Try All Valid Sequences**: Since dice are at most 2, and moves are limited, exhaustively generate all legal move sequences (with order H/L) and pick the one with highest score.
