The strategy for this policy is to implement a sophisticated Backgammon AI that prioritizes safety, blocking, and efficient bearing off while adhering strictly to the rules. The policy uses a hierarchical decision-making process:

1. **Bar Entry Priority**: If checkers are on the bar, prioritize entering them using the highest possible die to maximize flexibility and avoid leaving blots.
2. **Safe Moves First**: Avoid moves that leave a single checker (a blot) vulnerable to being hit unless there's no alternative or the move creates a strong block.
3. **Block Building**: Prioritize moves that create points (2+ checkers) in the opponent's home board or in key middle points to hinder their progress.
4. **Bearing Off Optimization**: When all checkers are in the home board, prioritize bearing off with the highest dice first to minimize the number of turns needed to finish.
5. **Dice Ordering Strategy**: Use 'H' (higher die first) when possible, as it often enables more aggressive or optimal moves; default to 'L' only when it leads to a significantly better position (e.g., bearing off or escaping a blot).
6. **Legal Move Validation**: Ensure every returned move is legal by exhaustive validation against game rules (bar entry, hitting, bearing off constraints).
7. **Search Strategy**: Since time is limited (1 second), we avoid deep lookahead and instead rely on hand-tuned heuristics that mimic expert human play, focusing on: safety, efficiency, and board control.

The policy evaluates all valid move combinations for the given dice, scores each based on a heuristic function, and returns the highest-scoring legal move.
