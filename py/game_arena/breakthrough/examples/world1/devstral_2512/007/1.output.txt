### Strategy for Breakthrough Policy

The strategy for this Breakthrough policy is based on a combination of aggressive forward movement and tactical capturing. The key principles are:

1. **Prioritize Winning Moves**: Always check if any piece can reach the opponent's home row (row 0 for black, row 7 for white) in the current move. If such a move exists, it is chosen immediately to win the game.

2. **Capture Opponent Pieces**: If no winning move is available, prioritize capturing opponent pieces. Capturing reduces the opponent's material and opens up paths for future advances. Diagonal captures are preferred as they also advance the piece forward.

3. **Advance Forward**: If no captures are available, move pieces forward (either straight or diagonally) to progress toward the opponent's home row. Preference is given to pieces that are closer to the opponent's home row to maximize progress.

4. **Avoid Blocking**: Ensure that moves do not block the path of other pieces, especially those closer to the opponent's home row. This is handled by checking the target square for friendlies before moving.

5. **Defensive Considerations**: While the primary focus is on advancing and capturing, the policy also avoids moving pieces into positions where they can be easily captured in the next turn (though this is not explicitly implemented due to time constraints).

This policy balances aggression with tactical awareness, aiming to win quickly while minimizing risks.
