The strategy for this policy is a hybrid approach combining minimax with alpha-beta pruning and heuristic evaluation to balance depth and efficiency within the 1-second time constraint. The evaluation function prioritizes:

1. **Progress toward victory**: Pieces closer to the opponent's home row are heavily weighted. For black (moving down), row 0 is the goal; for white (moving up), row 7 is the goal.
2. **Piece mobility**: More legal moves indicate better positioning.
3. **Structural integrity**: Avoid isolated pieces; reward clustered formations to prevent easy captures.
4. **Threat detection**: Prioritize captures that remove opponent pieces or block their advancement.
5. **Defensive awareness**: Prevent opponent breakthroughs by blocking their front-line pieces.

The search depth is adaptive: for mid-game positions, we search 3-4 plies; in endgame (few pieces), we go deeper (5-6 plies). We use transposition tables to cache evaluated positions and prune branches with poor material outcomes early. Legal moves are generated strictly according to the rules (straight forward to empty squares, diagonal forward to empty or opponent piece). The algorithm prioritizes capturing moves first to exploit immediate threats.

To ensure legal moves always return, we validate every generated move against the board state before returning. If no moves are found (should not happen per game rules), we fall back to a random legal move as safeguard.
