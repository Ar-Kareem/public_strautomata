To ensure the AI successfully navigates the Breakthrough game, it is essential to follow the movement rules precisely:

- **Straight forward move** is allowed only if the target square is empty.
- **Diagonal forward move** is allowed to either:
  - an empty square (non-capturing move), as per the initial rules.
  - or to a square occupied by an opponent's piece (capture), as per problem rules (bullet 3).

This means that diagonal moves are **always allowed**, either for non-capturing purposes or to capture an enemy piece.

---

### 🧠 Strategy Overview

The AI will follow a **three-phase evaluation strategy**:

1. **Promotion Moves** (highest priority): Any move that allows a pawn to reach the opponent's home row. This is a win condition and should be returned immediately.

2. **Capture Moves** (next priority): Capturing enemy pawns reduces their army and denies them potential promotions. Captures are prioritized over non-capturing moves.

3. **Non-Capturing Moves** (last resort): These are used when no promotions or captures are available. These moves aim to advance the pawns toward the opponent's home row.

---

### ✅ Implementation Logic

1. **Move Generation**: For each of our pawns:
   - Generate all **straight forward** moves if the target is empty.
   - Generate all **diagonal forward** moves:
     - If the target is **empty**, it's a non-capturing move.
     - If the target contains an **enemy piece**, it's a capture.

2. **Move Evaluation**:
   - First, scan all generated moves for those that **promote** (i.e., land in the opponent's home row).
   - Return the **first promotion move** found.
   - If no promoting move, look for **capture moves**.
   - If no captures exist, return the **first non-capturing move** (straight or diagonal).

### 🧵 Explanation of Strategy

- **Promotion Priority**: Any move that results in promotion is a **win**, so it is prioritized above all others.
- **Capture Priority**: Capturing reduces the opponent's material and improves our position.
- **Forward Movement**: If no promotion or capture is available, simply advance a pawn toward the promotion row.

---

### 🧪 Edge Cases Covered

- The code ensures **only legal moves** are considered by checking:
  - Bounds of the board.
  - Own cell exclusion.
  - Opponent piece exclusion (except in captures).
- It guarantees that the returned move is **always valid**, as required.
