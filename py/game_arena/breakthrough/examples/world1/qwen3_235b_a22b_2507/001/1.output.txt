The strategy for this policy is to implement a greedy, rule-based approach that prioritizes winning moves first, then focuses on forward progress while avoiding unnecessary risks. The policy evaluates all legal moves and orders them by desirability using the following hierarchy:

1. **Winning Move**: Any move that reaches the opponent's home row (row 0 for black, row 7 for white) is an immediate win and is returned right away.
2. **Capture Move**: Capturing an opponent piece is encouraged, especially if it opens up advancement paths.
3. **Forward Progress**: Move pieces as close as possible to the opponent's home row, prioritizing pieces that are already further advanced.
4. **Avoid Backward Risk**: Since pieces only move forward, we avoid moves that could unnecessarily expose multiple pieces.
5. **Tie-breaking**: When multiple moves are equally good, we use a simple heuristic (e.g., preferring central columns slightly, or advancing higher-ranked pieces) to pick one.

This policy uses minimal computation and does not require depth-limited search or heavy branching, thus staying well under the 1-second time limit. It is deterministic and very fast, making it suitable for a competitive arena.
