The strategy for this policy is to implement a greedy, heuristic-driven approach that balances aggressive advancement with tactical captures. The AI evaluates all legal moves and selects the best one based on a combination of:

1. **Winning immediately** — if any move reaches the opponent's home row, take it.
2. **Capturing opponent pieces** — prioritize captures, especially of pieces that are deep in our territory.
3. **Advancing pieces forward** — prefer moves that push pieces closer to the opponent's home row.
4. **Avoiding stagnation** — promote forward motion rather than lateral or backward ideas (not possible here) or idling.
5. **Piece promotion threat** — give extra value to pieces that are one step away from promotion.

The heuristic assigns scores to each possible move using weighted factors, including:
- Distance to opponent's back row (incentivize forward progress),
- Whether the move captures,
- Whether the move promotes immediately,
- Proximity of opponent pieces to our back row (to prioritize blocking if needed).

We use a simple but efficient board representation derived from `me` and `opp` to validate moves and compute outcomes.

The policy ensures legality by generating only valid moves, then scoring them. It defaults to safe advancement if no capture or win is available.
