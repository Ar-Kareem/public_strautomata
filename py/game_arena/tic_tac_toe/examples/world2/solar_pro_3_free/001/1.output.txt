The strategy for this policy prioritizes forced wins and blocks over open-line plays, leveraging the limited board size of 4x4 Tic Tac Toe. It implements a minimax-inspired approach with these key priorities:
1. **Immediate win check**: Examine all empty cells to identify positions that complete winning lines (two in a row with an empty third).
2. **Immediate block check**: Identify opponent's winning threats and block them first.
3. **Fork creation**: Detect opportunities to create two simultaneous winning threats that cannot both be blocked.
4. **Center control**: Prioritize center positions (1,1), (1,2), (2,1), (2,2) as they maximize line participation.
5. **Corner dominance**: Favor corner cells (0,0), (0,3), (3,0), (3,3) over edges due to their higher connectivity.
6. **Positional scoring**: Use a weighted scoring system based on line participation (corners participate in 3 lines, centers in 4, edges in 2).

This ensures robust play against all possible opponent strategies while adhering strictly to legal move constraints.
