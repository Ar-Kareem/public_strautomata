The strategy for this policy is to implement a combined approach that balances immediate tactical threats with long-term strategic positioning:

1. Immediate Win Check: If we can win with this move, take it.
2. Immediate Block: If opponent is about to win, block them.
3. Ring Detection: Try to build towards completing a ring, which is often the most reliable winning pattern.
4. Bridge/Fork Preparation: Identify moves that progress towards connecting corners or edges.
5. Pattern Expansion: Look for moves that expand existing groups in meaningful ways.
6. Strategic Growth: Prefer moves that create multiple viable paths to victory.
7. Connectivity: Prioritize moves that connect separate groups.
8. Central Control: Prefer moves closer to the center when no immediate tactical concerns exist.

The implementation looks for immediate tactical wins and blocks first, then evaluates positional moves based on their contribution to ring formation and connectivity before falling back to general strategic principles.
