### **Strategy for Havannah (15x15) Policy**

Havannah is a complex hexagonal board game where the goal is to create a **ring**, **bridge**, or **fork** before the opponent. Since the board is 15x15 (hexagonal grid), we need a policy that balances **defensive** and **offensive** moves while accounting for the **hexagonal adjacency rules**.

#### **Key Principles of the Policy:**
1. **Winning Moves First (Immediate Threat Detection):**
   - If placing a stone completes a **ring**, **bridge**, or **fork**, do it immediately.
   - Check all possible moves to see if they form a winning structure.

2. **Block Opponent’s Winning Moves (Defensive Priority):**
   - If the opponent has a **near-win** (one stone away from a ring, bridge, or fork), prioritize blocking it.
   - Use **BFS (Breadth-First Search)** to detect the shortest path to a winning structure.

3. **Expand Strongest Group (Offensive Expansion):**
   - If no immediate winning or blocking move exists, expand the player’s **largest connected group** (most stones in a single cluster).
   - This increases the chance of forming a winning structure while limiting the opponent’s opportunities.

4. **Center Control (Strategic Positioning):**
   - If the board is mostly empty, place stones in the **center** to maximize influence.
   - Avoid placing stones too close to edges unless necessary.

5. **Hexagonal Adjacency Handling:**
   - Since stones are placed on a hexagonal grid, adjacency must be checked in **6 directions** (not just 4 like in Go).
   - Use **BFS with hexagonal neighbors** to find connected groups and detect winning structures.

6. **Fallback to Valid Mask (Safety):**
   - If no winning or blocking move is found, pick a random valid move from `valid_mask`.

    queue.append((r, c))
    visited.add((r, c))

        connected.append((x, y))

### **Explanation of the Policy:**
1. **Winning Moves (`find_winning_move`):**
   - Checks if placing a stone at any valid position completes a **bridge**, **fork**, or **ring**.
   - Uses **BFS** to explore connected stones and verify winning conditions.

2. **Blocking Moves (`find_blocking_move`):**
   - If the opponent can win in their next move, this function identifies the best move to **block** them.
   - Simulates all possible opponent moves and checks if any would lead to an immediate win.

3. **Expanding Largest Group (`expand_group`):**
   - If no immediate win or block is found, the policy expands the **player’s largest connected group** (most stones in a single cluster).
   - This increases the chances of forming a winning structure while limiting the opponent’s opportunities.

4. **Fallback to Random Valid Move:**
   - If no strategic move is found, the policy selects a **random valid move** from the `valid_mask`.
