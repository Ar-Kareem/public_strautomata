The strategy for this policy is a heuristic-based approach that prioritizes immediate wins, blocks opponent wins, and scores moves based on connectivity, proximity to corners/edges, and opponent disruption. It first checks if any valid move completes a bridge (connecting two corners) or fork (connecting three edges). If not, it blocks opponent winning threats. Otherwise, it evaluates moves by:
1. **Connectivity**: Adjacent friendly stones increase score.
2. **Corner Proximity**: Closer to unoccupied corners enhances bridge potential.
3. **Edge Proximity**: Nearer to board edges improves fork chances.
4. **Blocking**: Adjacent opponent stones increase defense score.
5. **Central Control**: Early game focuses on central positioning for flexibility.

The policy uses BFS for connectivity checks and hex neighbor calculations for accurate adjacency. While ring detection is omitted for efficiency, the strategy balances offense and defense effectively.
