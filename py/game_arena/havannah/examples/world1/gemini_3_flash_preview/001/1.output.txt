### Strategy
The strategy for this Havannah AI (15x15) focuses on board-specific heuristics and efficient connectivity analysis. 

1.  **Hexagonal Topology**: The engine uses a defined neighbor function that accounts for the board's grid mapping:
    *   Same-column neighbors: `(r+1, c), (r-1, c)`
    *   Left-column neighbors: `(r, c-1), (r-1, c-1)`
    *   Right-column neighbors: `(r, c+1), (r+1, c+1)`
2.  **Move Prioritization**:
    *   **Winning Move**: If the player can complete a Bridge (connecting 2 corners), a Fork (connecting 3 edges), or a Ring (cycle) in one move, it plays there immediately.
    *   **Defensive Blocks**: If the opponent is one move away from completing a winning structure, the AI blocks that move.
    *   **Centrality & Connectivity**: If no immediate win/threat is detected, it prioritizes the center of the board to maximize potential connections to multiple edges and corners. It also favors moves that increase its "virtual" connectivity (extending existing chains).
3.  **Efficiency**: The policy uses a Disjoint Set Union (DSU) or Breadth-First Search (BFS) to evaluate connectivity conditions (Bridge/Fork) within the 1-second time limit per move.
